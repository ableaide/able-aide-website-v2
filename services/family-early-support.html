What we support

What family & early support can include

Supports are tailored to your child, your family and your routines — built to make everyday life just a little bit easier and more enjoyable.

Daily routines

Support around mornings, after school, evenings and weekends — aligned to therapy recommendations where appropriate and always led by your family's preferences.

Participation at home

Supporting your child to participate in play, chores, family activities and building independence step by step in a comfortable, familiar environment.

Community & social activities

Attending activities, programs, playgroups, extracurriculars, park visits and community events — with gentle, consistent support alongside your child.

School & early learning support

Supporting transitions to kindergarten, school or after-school programs — within scope and always with your consent and the school's collaboration.

Emotional regulation & calm support

Practising strategies from your therapy team to support emotional regulation, transitions and anxiety — in a way that's consistent, patient and non-pressuring.

Supporting parents & carers

Listening, sharing practical ideas and working with you to make supports sustainable in everyday family life — recognising that you know your child best.

Our approach

How we work with children & families

We know families are busy, tired and doing their best. Our role is to add support — not pressure.

Family-centred practice

We see you as the expert on your child. We work with you, not above you — listening to what matters and building supports around your family's real life.

Culturally responsive

We respect culture, language, faith and how your family understands care and support. We match workers who understand your community wherever possible.

Gentle & consistent workers

We focus on predictable, calm and consistent support — matched carefully to each child's personality, communication style and sensory preferences.

Therapist collaboration

With your consent, we work alongside OTs, speech pathologists, psychologists and educators to keep strategies consistent across all settings.

Flexible & responsive

Children grow and needs change. We stay flexible, check in regularly and adjust supports as your child develops and your family's circumstances evolve.

Built around real routines

Support is planned around what actually happens in your household — meals, bath time, school drop-off, prayer, outings — not ideal schedules on paper.

How it works

How we get started with your family

  1. 01

    We listen to your story

    We start by listening — to you, your child (in whatever way they communicate) and what everyday life really looks like for your family. No assumptions, no rushing.

  2. 02

    We learn your child & your routines

    Before any worker begins, we take time to understand your child's personality, preferences, communication style and what a good day looks like — then build support around that.

  3. 03

    Careful worker matching

    We match your child with workers who suit their personality, communication style and cultural background — not just whoever is available in your area.

  4. 04

    Support begins — gently

    We start slowly, check in regularly with you and adjust as your child settles in. Supports are always flexible and responsive to what's actually working.

Who it's for

Who family & early support is for

This service may be a good fit if you recognise your family in any of the following.

Families of young children

Early years or primary school-aged children with developmental, physical, sensory, intellectual or psychosocial disability — needing gentle, consistent support alongside family life.

Busy, stretched households

Parents and carers juggling multiple responsibilities who would benefit from practical, steady support that reduces pressure rather than adding to it.

Culturally diverse families

Households wanting support that honours language, culture, faith practices and family roles — with workers who genuinely understand your community and values.

Children in transition

Starting kindergarten, transitioning to primary school, moving into adolescence — we help children and families navigate key transitions with calm, consistent support.

Children with complex needs

Children with multiple support needs, complex behaviour profiles or co-occurring conditions — where support needs to be carefully coordinated with a therapy team.

Siblings & whole families

We recognise that disability affects the whole family. We work in ways that consider siblings, parents and carers — not just the participant.
