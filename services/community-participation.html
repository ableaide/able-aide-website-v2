Community Access & Social Support

We support NDIS participants to access the community, build independence, develop daily living skills and enjoy meaningful social participation. Whether it’s appointments, activities, hobbies or day-to-day tasks, Able Aide provides reliable, friendly and culturally aware support.

How we can support you

Examples of community participation include:

  • Attending medical, therapy or personal appointments
  • Shopping – groceries, personal items, errands
  • Social activities and outings
  • Public transport training and travel assistance
  • Exercise, walks, gym, swimming and recreation
  • Community events, groups, programs or clubs
  • Building independence in real-world settings
  • Accompanying you to educational or volunteering activities
  • Skill-building such as budgeting or planning trips

Who this service is for

Community support is ideal for NDIS participants who:

  • Want help getting out into the community safely
  • Are building independence and confidence
  • Need transport or mobility support
  • Prefer a consistent, friendly support worker

Able Aide supports participants across Victoria — including Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and regional areas.

Why community access matters

Getting out and about is essential for emotional wellbeing, independence and social connection. Our workers support you to build confidence and participate in community activities that matter to you.

Community support helps participants:

  • Increase social engagement
  • Build independence & decision-making skills
  • Improve confidence and mobility
  • Stay connected with culture, hobbies and interests
  • Attend important appointments

Able Aide understands the importance of cultural awareness, communication needs and personal boundaries. We take the time to match you with the right support worker who will respect your pace, comfort level and cultural identity.

Frequently asked questions

Can you help with transport?

Yes. We can assist with travel to appointments, activities or errands. Transport can be provided using public transport or your own NDIS-approved vehicle arrangements.

Can I choose my support worker?

We do our best to match you with a support worker based on your goals, interests, communication style and cultural preferences.

Do you help with social skills and confidence?

Absolutely. Community participation is a great way to build social confidence, independence and community engagement skills.

What areas do you service?

We support participants across Victoria, including Melbourne metro, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and nearby regional areas.
