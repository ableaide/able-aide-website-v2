Family & Early Support

Able Aide offers gentle, family-centred support for children, young people, parents and carers. Whether you need help with daily routines, building confidence, navigating appointments or creating supportive environments, we work alongside families to make life easier and more manageable.

How we support families

  • Help with school, kinder or daycare transitions
  • Routine-building at home (mornings, evenings, weekends)
  • Assisting children with emotional regulation and social skills
  • Supporting independence with daily tasks
  • Attending therapy or specialist appointments
  • Helping parents understand NDIS supports
  • Encouraging participation in community, play & learning
  • Coaching carers through daily challenges
  • Assisting young people to build confidence outside the home

Who this support is for

This service is ideal for families who:

  • Have a child or young person with NDIS funding
  • Need help with emotional, behavioural or social routines
  • Want support during key transitions (school, activities, therapy)
  • Prefer workers who understand cultural and family dynamics
  • Are seeking extra hands to support day-to-day life

We work with families across Victoria — including Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and surrounding regions.

Why early support matters

Early support is essential for building strong foundations. Children and young people develop skills through consistent routines, predictability and patient guidance. Able Aide’s family-focused approach empowers both the child and the care team.

Our support helps families strengthen:

  • Communication and emotional understanding
  • Social and play skills
  • Daily living independence
  • Capacity to handle challenging moments
  • Confidence in navigating the NDIS together

We understand that every family is unique. Cultural practices, family values, language and religious considerations all influence how support should look. Our team listens carefully so we can provide help that is meaningful, respectful and comfortable for you.

How Family & Early Support Works

1

Understand your family’s needs

We meet with you to talk about your child, routines, culture, strengths and challenges so we know how to best support you.

2

3

Begin supports

Your support worker assists with home routines, appointments, community access, transitions and confidence-building activities.

Frequently asked questions

Do you work with young children?

Yes. We support children of all ages, including early intervention participants, school-age children and young people.

Can you attend therapy appointments?

Yes — we can assist with attending sessions, helping with transitions, supporting communication with therapists and implementing strategies.

Do you help with school routines?

Absolutely. We support morning and after-school routines, including organisation, travel, emotional support and confidence-building.

What areas do you service?

We support families across all of Victoria, including Melbourne metro, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and nearby areas.
