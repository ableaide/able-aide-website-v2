Safe Transition Home After Hospital

How Able Aide supported a participant to safely return home after hospital with structured routines, mobility assistance and coordinated supports.

Names and identifying details changed for privacy.

Overview

“Michael”, a middle-aged NDIS participant living alone, was preparing for discharge after a medical event that significantly reduced his mobility. Hospital staff identified several risks including falls, medication management and daily living tasks.

His Support Coordinator referred him to Able Aide for an urgent and safe transition home.

Challenges

  • Returning home alone without adequate mobility support
  • Difficulty managing hygiene and meals post-surgery
  • High anxiety about falling or being alone
  • Multiple providers involved but no communication link
  • Needed support for equipment, transport and routines

What Able Aide Did

Immediate personal care

Daily hygiene, meal prep, mobility assistance and home setup for safety.

Coordinated supports

Liaised with hospital staff, physio and GP for safe discharge planning.

Routine rebuilding

Medication reminders, mobility schedule and structured morning routines.

Outcomes

  • Successful home transition with zero falls or incidents
  • Participant regained confidence and independence
  • Better attendance at medical and therapy appointments
  • Improved wellbeing and reduced anxiety
“I felt supported every step of the way. I wasn’t scared to come home anymore.”
