NDIS Goal Setting Guide

Clear, practical goal ideas to help your plan match your real life and priorities.

Why NDIS goals matter

Your goals guide what supports are considered relevant and how your plan is built. The most effective goals are specific, meaningful, and connected to your everyday routines and wellbeing.

What makes a good goal

  • Meaningful: reflects what the participant cares about
  • Practical: relates to daily living, participation, health or safety
  • Clear: describes what support will help improve
  • Flexible: doesn’t lock you into one method/provider

Goal examples (copy and adjust)

Daily living

  • “I want to improve my daily routine so I can feel more confident with personal care and meal preparation.”
  • “I want support to maintain my home environment safely and consistently.”

Community participation

  • “I want to build confidence to access the community and attend appointments and activities regularly.”
  • “I want support to develop social connections in ways that suit my interests and communication style.”

Health, wellbeing and safety

  • “I want to improve my wellbeing and manage stress through routine and structured supports.”
  • “I want a consistent support team to help me feel safe and reduce unexpected changes.”

Link goals to evidence

If you are preparing for a review, link goals to real examples: what is difficult now, how often, and what happens without support. If you need help preparing, see our NDIS review guide.
