What is Support Coordination?

Level 1 vs Level 2 — what's the difference?

Level 1 — Support Connection

Helps participants who are new to the NDIS — or who have straightforward support needs — to understand their plan, connect with providers and make the most of their funding.

Level 1 is typically a shorter-term engagement, focused on getting you set up, confident and connected.

Level 2 — Support Coordination

More intensive and ongoing coordination for participants with complex support needs — multiple providers, challenging situations or significant life changes. We resolve issues, coordinate services and help you build long-term capacity.

Level 2 also includes specialist support coordination for participants who need a qualified practitioner due to the complexity of their situation.

Both levels are funded under the Capacity Building budget in your NDIS plan. Not sure which level you have? Talk to us — we'll check your plan and explain.

What we provide

What Support Coordination includes

Practical help to set up, coordinate and maintain supports — so your plan works in real life, not just on paper.

Understanding your plan

We explain your funding categories, budgets and what you're entitled to — in plain language, without jargon or assumptions.

Connecting with providers

We help you find, engage and onboard the right providers for your goals — including providers who match your culture, language and communication style.

Coordinating supports

We keep your services organised and working together — managing schedules, resolving conflicts between providers and ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

Plan review preparation

We help you gather evidence, document outcomes and prepare for NDIS review conversations — so you're confident and well-prepared when it matters most.

Problem-solving & crisis support

When things don't go to plan — a provider cancels, a situation escalates, or a support breaks down — we step in quickly and help find solutions.

Building your independence

Our goal is to help you manage your own supports with confidence over time — not create dependency. We build your skills alongside your supports.

How it works

How we work with you

Clear steps, consistent communication and no surprises.

  1. 01

    Intake & fit check

    We start with a conversation — learning about your goals, current situation, communication preferences and what matters most to you. We confirm we're the right fit before anything is signed.

  2. 02

    Set up your supports

    We identify the right providers, help you engage them, clarify roles and responsibilities, and establish a clear schedule. We make sure everyone is on the same page.

  3. 03

    Coordinate & monitor

    We keep in regular contact — following up with providers, checking in with you, resolving issues early and adjusting supports as your needs evolve.

  4. 04

    Prepare for your review

    When your plan review approaches, we help you gather evidence of progress, document what's working and prepare for the conversation with confidence.

Who it's for

Who Support Coordination helps

New NDIS participants

Just received your plan and don't know where to start? We'll walk you through what everything means and get your supports set up clearly and quickly.

Participants with complex needs

Multiple providers, specialist services, behaviour support, mental health or housing — we coordinate the full picture so nothing is missed or duplicated.

Families & carers

Caring for a family member and feeling overwhelmed? We take on the coordination — communicating clearly with providers so you don't have to chase everyone yourself.

CALD communities

We support participants from all cultural and linguistic backgrounds — adapting our communication style, respecting cultural values and matching workers who understand your community.

People in transition

Leaving hospital, changing living arrangements or starting a new chapter — we help you navigate transitions smoothly with the right supports in place.

Young people

Goal planning, building independence and transitioning into adulthood — we support young participants to take increasing control of their own journey.
