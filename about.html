About Able Aide

Able Aide was founded to provide warm, reliable and person-centred disability support for NDIS participants and families across Victoria.

We believe that the right support can make daily life feel calmer, safer and more hopeful – not more complicated.

Your journey, our support

Behind every plan, there is a person, a family and a story. We take time to understand all three before we talk about rosters or tasks.

Talk to Able Aide

Our Story

Able Aide was created after years of seeing families and participants feel overwhelmed by the system, let down by inconsistent support, or unheard when they tried to explain what they really needed.

Our founder, Syeda, has over a decade of experience in disability support, support coordination, advocacy and direct care. Through this work, she saw the difference that:

  • Genuine listening
  • Clear communication
  • Consistency of workers
  • Respect for culture and family

can make in a person’s life. Able Aide grew from a desire to build a service where these things are not “nice-to-have”, but essential.

What matters to us

  • Listening first, then suggesting supports
  • Respecting your routines, culture and boundaries
  • Doing what we say we will do
  • Being honest about our capacity
  • Working with you, not just for you

Our Values in Practice

Respect

We respect your home, your family, your faith, your culture, your pronouns and your choices. We ask before we assist and we don’t make assumptions.

Reliability

We know how disruptive last-minute cancellations and constant staff changes can be. We aim for consistent workers and realistic scheduling.

Collaboration

We partner with you, your family, support coordinator, therapists and other providers. It’s a team effort – you’re at the centre of that team.

Experience You Can Trust

We bring together hands-on disability support experience, support coordination skills and a strong understanding of the NDIS.

NDIS support coordination

Experience supporting participants and families to understand plans, connect with right-fit providers, problem-solve issues and prepare for reviews.

Core supports & daily living

Practical experience providing personal care, domestic assistance, community access and family-focused supports in homes across Victoria.

Working with diverse communities

We have supported participants and families from many cultural, language and faith backgrounds, and we are always open to learning what is important to you.

How We Work With You

1

We listen

We take time to understand your goals, routines, worries, culture, faith and what “good support” means to you – not just what the paperwork says.

2

We plan together

We agree on supports, communication preferences, and how you’d like family members, carers or coordinators involved.

3

We keep checking in

As your needs and circumstances change, your support should too. We invite feedback and adjust where possible.
