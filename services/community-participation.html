What we support

What community participation can include

We support community access in ways that feel meaningful and safe — building confidence, routine and social connection aligned to your NDIS goals.

Appointments & essential trips

Medical, therapy, NDIS, Centrelink and community appointments — including planning, communication support and safe coordination.

Shopping & daily errands

Groceries, banking, post office, picking up medications and everyday tasks in the community — building confidence and independence over time.

Social & recreational activities

Programs, classes, sports, community centres, events, hobbies and interest groups — connecting you with activities you enjoy.

Faith, culture & community

Attending places of worship and cultural gatherings with genuine respect for your beliefs, practices and community traditions.

Skill-building in the community

Practising everyday skills like public transport, ordering food, time management and wayfinding — building independence step by step.

Goal-focused community access

Outings and activities aligned to your NDIS goals around independence, participation, social connection and community belonging.

Our approach

How we support you in the community

Steady, patient and always shaped by your comfort, goals and preferences.

  1. 01

    We understand your comfort levels

    We learn what feels safe, what feels challenging and what you'd like to build toward. No pressure to do more than you're ready for.

  2. 02

    We plan outings together

    Clear routines, transport planning, backup options and time to rest. We make community access predictable and low-stress.

  3. 03

    We build confidence step by step

    We start small if needed and build up gradually as confidence grows — always at your pace, never rushed.

Who it's for

Who community participation support helps

Adults

Building independence, routine and social connection in the community — at your pace, around your interests and goals.

Children & young people

Gentle support to attend programs, activities and social spaces — alongside families and carers, with age-appropriate guidance.

Culturally diverse participants

Community access that respects your culture, language, faith and community ties — with workers who understand your background.

Participants with anxiety

Calm, predictable support for participants who find community settings challenging — building confidence gradually at a pace that feels right.

Participants in SIL

Community participation complements SIL support — helping participants in supported living connect with the wider community outside the home.

Carers & families

Practical help so outings feel more manageable and consistent for the whole family — reducing carer burden while building participant confidence.
