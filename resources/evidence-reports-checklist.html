NDIS Evidence & Reports Checklist

What to gather before a plan review, reassessment, or change of circumstances request.

Start with practical examples

Clear, real-life examples are often the most helpful. Note what is difficult, how often it happens, and what changes when support is in place.

Checklist: common evidence types

  • Allied health reports: OT, speech pathology, psychology, physiotherapy (as relevant)
  • GP or specialist letters: particularly if health needs have changed
  • Support summaries: what supports are delivered and why they are needed
  • Behaviour support information: if applicable (triggers, safety, strategies)
  • School / education input: for children and young people where relevant
  • Daily routine notes: personal care, meals, sleep, mobility, community access

Evidence tips (simple but effective)

  • Use everyday language; avoid over-technical wording if it is not natural for you.
  • Explain impact on function (what the participant can/can’t do without support).
  • Include frequency and safety risks where relevant.
  • Keep documents organised and labelled by date.

If you are preparing for a review, see our NDIS Plan Review Guide.
