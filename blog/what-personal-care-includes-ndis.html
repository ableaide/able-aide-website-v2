What Personal Care Includes Under the NDIS

Personal care is one of the most important supports in an NDIS plan. It helps people live safely, comfortably and independently at home and in their community.

This guide explains exactly what personal care covers, who it’s for, and how Able Aide delivers it with dignity and respect.

Need personal care support?

Our team provides warm, respectful support with daily routines, at your pace and your comfort level.

Personal care services Talk to our team

What does “personal care” mean?

Personal care is support with daily tasks that help you look after your body, health and hygiene. It’s funded under your NDIS plan when these tasks are difficult to manage independently due to a disability.

The goal is simple: to help you live safely, maintain your wellbeing and continue routines that matter to you.

What personal care includes (NDIS approved)

✔ Showering & bathing support

  • Assistance with washing, hair care and hygiene
  • Setting up safe bathroom routines
  • Supporting independence where possible

✔ Dressing & grooming

  • Choosing clothes
  • Dressing / undressing
  • Brushing hair, shaving and grooming

✔ Toileting & continence support

  • Using the toilet safely
  • Changing continence aids
  • Maintaining hygiene and privacy

✔ Mealtime support

  • Assistance preparing simple meals
  • Help with feeding where appropriate
  • Support following swallowing/texture plans (if provided by a professional)

✔ Mobility & transfers

  • Getting in and out of bed
  • Use of hoists, slide sheets and mobility aids
  • Safe movement around the home

✔ Medication prompts (non-clinical)

Support workers can provide reminders but cannot administer medications that require clinical skills.

✔ Daily routines & capacity-building

  • Morning and evening routines
  • Developing independence where possible
  • Practising safe home skills

Who is personal care for?

NDIS personal care supports participants who:

  • Have physical disabilities
  • Have developmental or cognitive disabilities
  • Need assistance due to mental health conditions
  • Have sensory or neurological conditions
  • Are children with early childhood needs

Support is flexible — from 30 minutes to several hours, morning or night, 7 days a week.

How Able Aide provides personal care

We take a warm, consistent and respectful approach to personal care. Our workers:

  • Respect your privacy, culture and communication style
  • Go at your pace — never rushing
  • Maintain dignity at all times
  • Follow safety plans and clinical instructions
  • Support independence wherever possible

Personal care is more than tasks — it’s about trust, comfort and feeling safe with the person supporting you.

How personal care fits into your NDIS plan

It is usually funded under:

  • Core Supports – Daily Activities
  • Sometimes Capacity Building – Daily Living (for training, skill-building or routines)

If you’re unsure how your funding works, we can help explain it clearly.
