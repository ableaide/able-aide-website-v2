Family & Early Support

When a young child has additional needs, the whole family feels it. Routines, sleep, school, siblings, appointments, work and emotions can all become more complex. Our role is to walk alongside you with calm, practical support.

Able Aide provides gentle, family-centred support for children and families with NDIS plans across Victoria.

Support for the whole family, not just the plan

We know you are doing your best. Our approach is compassionate, realistic and tailored — focused on building routines, participation and confidence, not perfection.

Talk about support for your family

Early Support That Fits Everyday Life

Early support works best when it fits into real life — morning chaos, school runs, tired evenings, cultural and faith commitments, and everything in between. We focus on small, meaningful changes that make family life feel a little calmer and more manageable.

We work closely with parents, carers and other key people in a child’s life. Together, we look at what is working, where the pressure points are, and how we can gently support your child’s participation, communication and development within your family’s own values and routines.

Ways We Can Support Your Child and Family

Daily Routines

Support with creating and carrying out calmer daily rhythms, such as:

  • Morning/getting ready routines
  • After-school or after-kinder structures
  • Evening and bedtime routines
  • Meal and snack times
  • Transitions between activities or environments

Participation & Community Access

Helping your child feel more confident and included, including:

  • Attending kinder, school or appointments
  • Going to parks, libraries or local activities
  • Joining playgroups or social programs
  • Supporting participation in cultural or faith settings
  • Building comfort in new or busy environments

Supporting Parents & Carers

Walking alongside you as you navigate the NDIS and everyday life:

  • Helping you understand and use NDIS funding
  • Supporting implementation of therapy recommendations
  • Providing practical ideas that fit your family’s values
  • Being a calm, listening ear when things feel heavy

How We Work With Families

We understand that families come in all shapes, cultures, languages and structures. Our approach is flexible and led by your priorities.

1

We Listen to Your Story First

We take time to hear about your child, your family, your culture and faith, your routines, and your biggest worries and hopes. You are the expert in your child.

2

We Focus on Small, Doable Steps

Instead of unrealistic checklists, we look for one or two changes that could make a concrete difference to daily life — and build from there as you feel ready.

3

We Work as a Team Around Your Child

With your consent, we can liaise with therapists, educators, Support Coordinators and others to help keep supports consistent and aligned.

Which Families We Support

This service can be helpful for families who:

  • Have a young child with an NDIS plan or early childhood funding
  • Feel overwhelmed by routines, behaviours or appointments
  • Are trying to balance siblings’, work and school needs
  • Want support that respects culture, faith and parenting style
  • Would like help putting therapy ideas into everyday life

Funding & Locations

  • Supports funded through NDIS Core and/or Capacity Building (as appropriate)
  • Self-managed and plan-managed participants
  • Families in Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and regional Victoria

If you’re unsure whether your child’s funding can be used for this type of support, we’re happy to talk through it with you.

Family & Early Support – Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the same as early childhood intervention?

Our service is complementary to early childhood intervention. We are not therapists; we provide practical, family-focused support to help you implement strategies and routines in everyday life, alongside any therapy your child receives.

Do you work directly with children or mainly with parents?

Both. We spend time with children during routines and activities, and we also work closely with parents and carers, as they know their child best and are key to long-term change.

Can you help if we haven’t started with NDIS yet?

We can offer general guidance and support you to understand the process, but formal NDIS supports require an approved plan. We can still listen and offer suggestions about where to start.

Will you replace my child’s therapists or educators?

No. Our role is to complement, not replace, existing professionals. We help everyone work together around your child and family.

Do you provide behaviour support?

We are not a behaviour support provider. However, we can support families to follow strategies recommended by appropriately qualified practitioners, where it is safe and within scope.

Can you support siblings too?

While NDIS funding is focused on the participant, we recognise that siblings are part of the family system. We consider their needs when planning routines and supports.

Do you respect different parenting styles and cultures?

Absolutely. We actively listen to your values, culture, language and faith, and work with you to find approaches that feel respectful and realistic for your family.

Can you attend school or kinder meetings?

In some cases, yes — where appropriate and funded, and with your consent, we may attend relevant meetings to support communication and planning.

How often will you visit?

This depends on your child’s needs, your goals and your NDIS funding. Some families prefer weekly visits; others prefer less frequent, but longer or more targeted support. We decide this together.

How do we get started?

You can contact us directly or speak with your Support Coordinator or plan manager. We will talk through your situation, funding and goals, and outline possible next steps in a gentle, no-pressure way.

Need Support for Your Child and Family?

If you’re feeling tired, stretched or unsure what to do next, you’re not alone. We’re here to listen and explore options with you at your pace.
