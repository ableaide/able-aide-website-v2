About Able Aide

Able Aide was founded to provide warm, reliable, person-centred support for NDIS participants and families across Victoria.

We believe the right support should make daily life feel calmer, safer and more hopeful — not more complicated.

Your journey, our support

Behind every plan is a person, a family and a story. We take time to understand all three before we talk about rosters or tasks.

Talk to Able Aide

Our Story

Able Aide was created after years of seeing families and participants feel overwhelmed, let down by inconsistent support, or unheard when trying to explain what they needed.

Our founder, Syeda, has over a decade of experience in disability support, support coordination, advocacy and direct care. She saw firsthand that:

  • Genuine listening
  • Clear communication
  • Consistency of workers
  • Respect for culture and family

These values profoundly impact a person's confidence, wellbeing and sense of safety. Able Aide was created to ensure they are foundational — not optional.

What matters to us

  • Listening first, then suggesting supports
  • Respecting your routines, culture and boundaries
  • Doing what we say we will do
  • Being honest about our capacity
  • Working with you, not just for you

Our Values in Practice

Respect

We respect your home, culture, family, faith, pronouns and choices. We don’t make assumptions — we ask before we assist.

Reliability

We know how disruptive last-minute cancellations or shifts in workers can be. We prioritise consistency and honest communication.

Collaboration

We work alongside you, your family, your coordinator, therapists and providers. Support works best when everyone works together.

Experience You Can Trust

We bring together disability support experience, support coordination skills, and a strong understanding of the NDIS.

NDIS support coordination

Experience helping participants navigate plans, coordinate services, problem-solve issues and prepare for reviews.

Core supports & daily living

Years of providing personal care, community access, domestic tasks and family-focused supports across Victoria.

Working with diverse communities

We support participants from many cultural, language and faith backgrounds — always taking time to understand what matters to you.

How We Work With You

1

We listen

We take time to understand your goals, routines, culture, concerns and what “good support” looks like — not just what's written in your plan.

2

We plan together

We work with you, your family and your coordinator (if you have one) to build supports that fit your life.

3

We keep checking in

Your needs may change — your support should too. We check in regularly and adjust where possible.
