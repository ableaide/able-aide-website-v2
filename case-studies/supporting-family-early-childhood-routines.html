Supporting a Family With Early Childhood Routines

How Able Aide helped a family create calmer mornings, smoother transitions and stronger communication for their young child.

Names changed for privacy.

Overview

“Sara”, a 5-year-old NDIS participant, struggled with morning routines, emotional regulation and transitioning to kinder. The family felt overwhelmed and unsure which strategies to use consistently.

Challenges

  • Frequent meltdowns in morning routines
  • Difficulty with transitions (home → car → kinder)
  • Limited communication and shared attention
  • Parents overwhelmed with therapy recommendations
  • Inconsistent routines affecting the whole family

What Able Aide Did

Visual routine charts

Simple, predictable steps for mornings and evenings.

Gentle transitions

Timers, warnings and calm phrasing to reduce overwhelm.

Play-based engagement

Strengthening shared attention and early communication.

Outcomes

  • Calmer mornings & improved participation
  • Child attending kinder with fewer meltdowns
  • Parents confident using strategies daily
  • Better communication between family & therapists
“The support worker made our home calmer. We now have a routine that actually works.”
