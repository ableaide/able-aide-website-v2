How to Access Allied Health Through the NDIS

Occupational therapy, speech therapy, psychology and physiotherapy can make a huge difference ‚Äî but navigating NDIS processes, referrals, waitlists and funding can feel overwhelming.

This guide explains how to access allied health step-by-step using simple, clear language.

What is ‚Äúallied health‚Äù under the NDIS?

Allied health refers to a range of therapists who support participants with communication, mobility, daily living, emotional regulation and wellbeing.

  • Occupational therapy (OT)
  • Speech therapy
  • Physiotherapy
  • Psychology
  • Behaviour support
  • Dietitian
  • Exercise physiology
  • Functional and diagnostic assessments

What funding categories cover allied health?

  • Improved Daily Living (most common)
  • Improved Relationships
  • Improved Health & Wellbeing
  • Core funding (in some circumstances)

If you‚Äôre unsure, a support coordinator can help explain which budgets apply.

How to access allied health (step-by-step)

1. Identify your goals

  • Communication development
  • Mobility or strength
  • Emotional regulation
  • Building independence at home

2. Decide which therapy fits your goals

Example: OT for daily living skills, speech therapy for communication, psychology for mental health.

3. Contact therapy providers

  • Explain your goals
  • Check availability & waitlists
  • Check if they travel to your area
  • Confirm if they accept your funding type

4. Compare wait times

Many clinics have long waits ‚Äî sometimes months.

Able Aide can assist:

  • Contacting multiple clinics
  • Finding shorter waitlists
  • Coordinating assessments
  • Ensuring reports meet NDIS standards

5. Begin therapy

The therapist will complete an assessment, set goals and begin ongoing sessions.

6. Review and adjust as needed

  • Switch therapists if not the right fit
  • Request culturally matched workers
  • Ask for home visits if required

Do you need a referral?

No. Most allied health does not require a GP or specialist referral unless it is a medical assessment.
