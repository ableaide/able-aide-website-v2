Community Access & Social Participation

We help you get out into the community—whether for appointments, social events, shopping, exercise, hobbies, faith activities, family visits or simply enjoying your day.

Our goal is to support you in feeling confident, connected and included.

Support that fits your goals

Whether you want to build independence, reduce anxiety, or simply participate more, we support you at your pace—never rushed, never pressured.

How We Support You in the Community

Community access means more than going from point A to point B. It’s about feeling confident, reducing stress, practicing skills, and staying connected with the people and activities that matter to you.

Appointments & errands

We provide support to attend medical appointments, therapy sessions, school or TAFE, job interviews, or NDIS meetings. We stay with you if needed to take notes, translate information, or provide reassurance.

Shopping & community tasks

Support with grocery shopping, banking, post office visits, pharmacy pick-ups, or running errands— helping you build skills and independence along the way.

Social and recreational outings

We help you enjoy local activities like sports, cafés, gyms, community classes, libraries, markets, concerts or nature walks. Supports can be regular or occasional.

Confidence-building

If you feel anxious or overwhelmed in public spaces, we work together to gradually build confidence through paced exposure, planning and reassurance.

Transport support

Assistance using public transport, taxis (with funding), rideshare or transport arranged by family. We help with route planning and safety.

Friendship & connection

Some participants simply want kind company during outings. We are happy to be that familiar, encouraging support person.

How We Plan Community Supports

1

We talk about your goals

Perhaps you want to join a gym, attend therapy regularly, visit family more often, or build skills for independent travel. We start with what matters to you.

2

We plan around your comfort

We consider sensory needs, cultural preferences, confidence levels, fatigue, public environments, travel time and safety.

3

We support at your pace

Some days you may want a shorter outing, a quieter location or reassurance. We adjust flexibly to how you’re feeling on the day.

Who Benefits Most From Community Access?

This support is ideal for people who want to:

  • Be more independent outside the home
  • Build social and communication skills
  • Attend appointments but need reassurance
  • Reduce anxiety in public areas
  • Stay active, connected and engaged
  • Learn how to use transport safely

We support participants of all ages and abilities, including people with autism, psychosocial disabilities, physical disabilities, intellectual disabilities and chronic illness.
