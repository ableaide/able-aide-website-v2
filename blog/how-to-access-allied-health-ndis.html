How to Access Allied Health Through the NDIS

Occupational therapy, speech therapy, psychology and physiotherapy can make a huge difference ‚Äî but navigating NDIS processes, referrals, waitlists and funding can feel overwhelming.

This guide explains how to access allied health step-by-step using simple, clear language.

What is ‚Äúallied health‚Äù under the NDIS?

Allied health refers to a range of therapists who support participants with communication, mobility, daily living, emotional regulation and wellbeing.

  • Occupational therapy (OT)
  • Speech therapy
  • Physiotherapy
  • Psychology
  • Behaviour support
  • Dietitian
  • Exercise physiology
  • Functional and diagnostic assessments

What funding categories cover allied health?

  • Improved Daily Living (most common)
  • Improved Relationships
  • Improved Health & Wellbeing
  • Core funding (in some circumstances)

If you‚Äôre unsure, a support coordinator can help explain which budgets apply.

How to access allied health (step-by-step)

1. Identify your goals

  • Communication development
  • Mobility or strength
  • Emotional regulation
  • Building independence at home

2. Decide which therapy fits your goals

Example: OT for daily living skills, speech therapy for communication, psychology for mental health, physio for movement, pain or strength.

3. Check your plan type and budgets

  • Plan-managed: you can use registered and non-registered providers
  • Self-managed: you can use most providers and pay invoices directly
  • NDIA-managed: you‚Äôll generally need NDIS registered providers

4. Contact therapy providers

  • Explain your goals and preferred session type (clinic / home / telehealth)
  • Check availability & waitlists
  • Confirm service area and travel fees (if any)
  • Confirm their rates and how they invoice

5. Compare wait times

Many clinics have long waits ‚Äî sometimes months.

Able Aide can assist by:

  • Contacting multiple providers on your behalf
  • Finding shorter waitlists where possible
  • Coordinating assessment bookings
  • Helping you understand therapy recommendations
  • Ensuring reports align with your goals and NDIS needs

6. Start therapy and set a plan

The therapist will usually begin with an assessment, then agree on goals, frequency and what progress looks like.

7. Review and adjust

  • Change approach or frequency if it‚Äôs not helping
  • Switch therapists if it‚Äôs not the right fit
  • Request culturally safe or language-matched supports where possible
  • Ask for home visits if travel is a barrier

Do you need a referral?

Usually no ‚Äî most allied health providers accept self-referrals. A GP referral may be required for some medical assessments or other funding pathways (not the NDIS).

What to ask a therapist before you start

  • Do you have experience with my disability / age group?
  • Do you offer home visits or telehealth?
  • What is your wait time for the first appointment?
  • How do you set goals and measure progress?
  • Can you provide reports for plan reviews if needed?
  • What are your fees, cancellation policy and travel charges?

How Able Aide supports allied health access

  • Finding suitable therapists based on goals, location and preferences
  • Coordinating appointments and communication between providers
  • Organising assessments and supporting follow-through at home
  • Supporting CALD families and cultural safety needs
  • Helping you understand reports and next steps
