Family & Early Childhood Support

Early years come with big changes — new routines, appointments, behaviour shifts, school transitions and emotional ups and downs. It can be a lot for parents and caregivers to navigate alone.

We provide gentle, practical NDIS support to families of young children across Victoria, helping with routines, early development, behaviour, communication and participation at home, school and in the community.

Support for your child — and your whole family

We know that when a child needs support, the whole family feels it. Our approach is calm, respectful and family-centred, working alongside parents, carers and therapists.

Helping Everyday Life Feel More Manageable

Many families tell us they feel pulled in every direction — managing school, therapies, behaviour, siblings, appointments and daily routines. We help make home life calmer, more predictable and easier to navigate.

Our support focuses on what daily life actually looks like — mornings, mealtimes, after-school routines, community outings, transitions and emotional regulation — not just what happens in appointment rooms.

What We Can Support With

Daily Routines & Transitions

We help families create calmer, more predictable routines:

  • Morning & after-school routines
  • Bedtime & wind-down routines
  • Mealtime support (within scope)
  • Preparing for appointments or outings
  • Transition support between activities
  • Quiet-time and regulation strategies

Early Development & Participation

Practical support for early childhood skills:

  • Play-based engagement & shared attention
  • Basic communication & social interaction
  • Following simple routines & instructions
  • Using therapy strategies recommended by OT or speech
  • Building confidence in kinder or school settings

Behaviour & Emotional Regulation

Supporting children through big emotions and changes:

  • Understanding behaviour patterns & triggers
  • Using calming or sensory strategies (as advised)
  • Reducing overwhelm through environment adjustments
  • Introducing simple visual supports
  • Following behaviour support plans (within scope)

Our Family-Centred Approach

We see families as the experts in their own children. Our role is to listen, understand and offer practical, realistic support that fits your values and routines.

Listening First

We take time to understand your child’s needs, what’s working, what’s difficult and what you’d love to see change — without judgement.

Working Alongside You

We build on your existing strategies and therapy input, offering small achievable steps rather than overwhelming plans.

Respecting Culture & Family Values

Every family has its own culture, language, faith and parenting style. We adapt support so it feels culturally safe and aligned with your values.

Who This Service Is For

This support may be right for your family if:

  • Your child has an NDIS plan (early childhood or primary years)
  • You feel overwhelmed by routines or behaviour
  • You’re juggling therapy, school and home life
  • You need help using therapy strategies consistently
  • Siblings are affected by routines or behaviour
  • You want support that includes the whole family

Funding & Locations

  • Supports funded under Core or Capacity Building
  • Self-managed & plan-managed participants
  • Families across Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton & regional Victoria

If you’re unsure whether your child’s plan can fund this support, we can explain your options.

Family & Early Support – Frequently Asked Questions

Are you a therapy service?

No. We are not a clinical therapy service. We work alongside your child’s therapists to help families use their recommendations in daily life.

Do you replace therapists?

No — we complement therapy by supporting day-to-day routines, not clinical intervention.

Can you help with behaviours of concern?

Yes — we can follow behaviour support strategies from qualified practitioners, within our scope. Complex behaviour requires specialist input.

Do you support very young children?

Yes — including children in early childhood NDIS pathways.

Do you support siblings?

Yes — while NDIS funds the participant, we consider siblings when designing routines and support strategies.

Do you work with schools or kinders?

With your consent, yes. We support communication and consistent strategies across home, school and therapy.

Is support available after school hours?

Where capacity allows — many families prefer late afternoon support.

Can you help prepare for NDIS reviews?

Yes — we help gather information, reflect on progress and prepare examples.

How is this funded?

Usually under Core or Capacity Building, depending on goals. We can help interpret your child’s plan.

How do we start?

Contact us directly or ask your Support Coordinator or therapist to refer. We’ll talk through your situation and next steps.
