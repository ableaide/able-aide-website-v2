Family & Early Support

We provide gentle, practical support for young children on the NDIS and their families, focusing on daily routines, participation, confidence and early development.

We know caring for a child with additional needs can be rewarding, busy and exhausting. You don’t have to do it alone.

Support that wraps around your family

We listen to your priorities as parents or carers and work with you, your child and your therapy team to support everyday life – not just appointments.

Talk about early support

How We Support Children & Families

Our family & early support is flexible. It can include hands-on support with the child, help with routines and participation, and collaboration with parents, carers and therapists.

Daily routines

Support during key times of the day—mornings, after school, weekends or evenings— to help things run more smoothly, whether that’s getting dressed, bathing, mealtimes or bedtime.

Participation in activities

Supporting your child to participate in playgroups, kinder, early learning, community activities, sports, religious or cultural events where appropriate.

Skill-building at their pace

Encouraging independence in small steps: washing hands, packing a bag, making simple choices, practicing communication, or following simple routines with lots of encouragement.

Supporting parents & carers

Sometimes support looks like having an extra pair of hands, another calm adult in the room, or someone to help share the load while you focus on other family needs.

Working with therapists

We can reinforce strategies from OTs, speech pathologists, psychologists or other therapists—such as visuals, communication tools, sensory strategies and behaviour supports (within our role).

Respect for culture & family values

We recognise that every family has its own culture, values, traditions and parenting styles. We work with you, not against you, to support your child in a way that fits your family.

What Working Together Can Look Like

1

We listen to your story

You know your child best. We ask about their strengths, challenges, likes and dislikes, and what life is like at home, school or kinder.

2

We focus on your priorities

Instead of telling you what should matter, we ask what actually matters to your family—less stress at bedtime, smoother mornings, confident outings, or more time for siblings.

3

We provide consistent support

As much as possible, we aim to provide familiar workers and consistent routines so your child feels safe, understood and supported over time.

Who Is Family & Early Support For?

This support suits families with young children on the NDIS who would benefit from practical, home- and community-based support.

  • Children who need extra support with everyday routines
  • Families juggling multiple appointments and responsibilities
  • Parents seeking calm, patient support workers for their child
  • Children who find community outings stressful or overwhelming
  • Families wanting help implementing therapy strategies at home

We take a whole-family view: what helps the child should also help the household. Our workers aim to be reliable, kind and respectful – not another source of stress.
