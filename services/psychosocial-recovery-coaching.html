What is PRC?

Understanding Psychosocial Recovery Coaching

What a Psychosocial Recovery Coach does

A Psychosocial Recovery Coach (PRC) is a specialist NDIS support worker who helps people living with mental health conditions navigate their recovery journey. They work alongside you — not above you — to help you:

  • Set meaningful recovery goals
  • Build daily living and self-management skills
  • Understand and use your NDIS plan effectively
  • Connect with mental health, community and peer support services
  • Develop resilience and confidence over time

What makes PRC different

Unlike a Support Coordinator (who focuses on your NDIS plan and providers), a Recovery Coach focuses specifically on your mental health recovery journey — your daily life, your goals, your resilience and your connection to others.

PRC is grounded in recovery-oriented practice — recognising that recovery is personal, non-linear and about living a meaningful life, not just managing symptoms.

Many participants have both a Recovery Coach and a Support Coordinator. They work alongside each other and are both fundable in your NDIS plan at the same time.

How we support you

What Psychosocial Recovery Coaching can include

Recovery coaching is flexible and shaped around your goals — not a fixed program.

Setting recovery goals

Working with you to identify what a meaningful, connected life looks like — and building a realistic plan to work toward it, one step at a time.

Daily living skills

Building practical skills around routines, self-care, cooking, managing a home, getting to appointments and handling daily tasks with more confidence.

Understanding your NDIS plan

Helping you understand your plan, your funding and how to use your supports effectively — in plain language, without the overwhelm.

Connecting with services

Identifying and connecting with mental health services, peer support, community programs and other supports that complement your recovery.

Building resilience & coping

Developing strategies to manage setbacks, stress and difficult periods — building the inner resources to navigate life's challenges more confidently over time.

Community participation

Helping you reconnect with community — social activities, interest groups, volunteering, work or study — at whatever level feels right for you right now.

Housing & daily stability

Supporting you to maintain stable housing, manage tenancy responsibilities and build the daily routines that support recovery and wellbeing.

Crisis prevention planning

Working together on a wellness and crisis plan — identifying early warning signs and building a personal safety plan that reflects your preferences.

Family & carer support

With your consent, including family members and carers in conversations about how they can best support your recovery without adding pressure.

Our approach

How we work with you

Our approach to recovery coaching is grounded in respect, patience and walking alongside — not directing.

Trauma-informed practice

We recognise that many people living with mental health conditions have experienced trauma. Our Recovery Coaches are trained to work in ways that feel safe, predictable and non-judgmental — at all times.

Culturally safe support

Recovery looks different across cultures, families and communities. We adapt our approach to honour your cultural background, language, faith and the way your family understands wellbeing and recovery.

Recovery-oriented, not symptom-focused

We focus on what matters to you — meaning, connection, contribution and quality of life — not just symptom management. Recovery is about living well, not just being well.

Strengths-based

We start from what you're already good at, what you've already survived, and what matters most to you — building from your strengths, not your deficits.

Who PRC is for

Is Psychosocial Recovery Coaching right for you?

PRC may be a good fit if you recognise yourself or someone you know in any of the following.

Living with a mental health condition

You have an NDIS plan that recognises your psychosocial disability — and you want support that focuses specifically on your mental health recovery, not just your disability supports.

Feeling stuck or overwhelmed

Recovery has stalled, daily life feels unmanageable, or you're struggling to move forward even with existing supports in place. A Recovery Coach can help you identify what's getting in the way and build momentum.

Recently discharged or in transition

You've recently left hospital, a residential program, or another intensive support — and you need help building stable routines and reconnecting with community at your own pace.

Wanting to build independence

You want to do more on your own — manage your home, maintain relationships, pursue work or study or community involvement — and need support to build those skills step by step.

Navigating the NDIS with mental health

The NDIS system can feel overwhelming, especially when you're already managing your mental health. A Recovery Coach can help you understand and use your plan without adding to your stress.

Family members & carers

You care for someone living with a mental health condition and want support to understand how best to help — without taking over. With your loved one's consent, we can include you in the recovery process.

NDIS funding

How PRC is funded in your NDIS plan

Funding category

Psychosocial Recovery Coaching is funded under the Capacity Building — Improved Daily Living budget in your NDIS plan. It uses the support category "Improved Daily Living Skills".

It is distinct from Core Supports — so having PRC funded does not reduce your Core budget for other supports like personal care or community participation.

Getting PRC into your plan

To access PRC funding, the NDIA needs to determine it is reasonable and necessary for your situation. This typically requires:

  • A confirmed psychosocial disability (mental health condition)
  • Evidence of functional impact on daily life
  • A clear rationale for why PRC would benefit your recovery

Your Support Coordinator, treating psychiatrist or GP can help provide this evidence. Talk to us if you need guidance →

Many participants have both — they serve different but complementary roles.

Support Coordination

Focuses on your NDIS plan — setting up providers, coordinating services, managing plan reviews and making sure all your funded supports are working together.
