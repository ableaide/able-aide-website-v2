Family & Early Childhood Support

Early years are full of big changes — new diagnoses, appointments, school transitions, behaviour changes and emotional ups and downs. It can be a lot for children, parents and carers to manage alone.

Able Aide provides gentle, practical support to families of children with NDIS plans across Victoria, focusing on daily routines, early development, behaviour, participation and confidence at home and in the community.

Support for both the child and the family

We know that when a child needs support, the whole family feels it. Our approach is family-centred – we work alongside parents, carers and other supports, not instead of them.

Talk about family & early support

Helping Everyday Life Feel More Manageable

Many families tell us they feel pulled in different directions – juggling school, therapies, siblings, behaviour, appointments and daily tasks. Our Family & Early Support service is designed to reduce stress, build routines and support children’s participation in real-life settings, not just in appointment rooms.

We do this by focusing on what daily life actually looks like in your home — mornings, evenings, mealtimes, homework, appointments, play time, transitions and meltdowns. Together, we work out small adjustments and consistent strategies that feel realistic for your family to use.

What We Can Support With

Daily Routines & Transitions

We help families create calm, predictable routines around:

  • Morning and after-school routines
  • Bedtime and sleep wind-down routines
  • Mealtimes and food-related challenges (within scope)
  • Getting ready for appointments or outings
  • Supporting transitions between activities
  • Breaks and quiet-time strategies

Early Development & Participation

Practical support around early skills and participation, such as:

  • Play-based engagement and shared attention
  • Basic communication and interaction practice
  • Following simple routines and instructions
  • Supporting therapy strategies recommended by your OT or speech therapist
  • Building confidence in kinder, early schooling or community settings

Behaviour & Emotional Regulation

We support families to respond calmly and consistently by:

  • Understanding triggers and patterns in behaviour
  • Using agreed calming or sensory strategies (as advised by therapists)
  • Adjusting the environment to reduce overwhelm
  • Using simple visual supports or prompts (where appropriate)
  • Supporting safety plans developed with clinicians (within scope)

Our Family-Centred Approach

We see families as the experts in their own children. Our role is to listen, understand and offer practical support that respects your culture, values, routines and parenting style.

Listening First

We take time to hear your story — what’s going well, what’s hard, what your child enjoys, and what you hope will feel different. We recognise the emotional load that comes with caring for a child with additional needs.

Working Alongside You

We don’t come in with judgement or quick fixes. We work with your existing strategies and team, and suggest small, achievable changes rather than overwhelming plans.

Respecting Culture & Family Dynamics

Every family has its own culture, faith, language and ways of doing things. We respect this and adapt our approach so support feels culturally safe and aligned with your values.

Who This Service Is For

Our Family & Early Support service may be a good fit if:

  • Your child has an NDIS plan and is in early childhood or primary years
  • You’re feeling overwhelmed by daily routines or behaviour
  • You’re trying to juggle home life, appointments and school
  • You’re unsure how to use therapy suggestions in real life
  • There are siblings who are also affected by routines or behaviour
  • You’d like support that includes the whole family, not just the child

Funding & Locations

  • Supports typically funded under Core or Capacity Building, depending on goals
  • Self-managed and plan-managed NDIS participants
  • Families across Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton & regional Victoria

If you’re unsure whether your child’s plan can fund this type of support, we’re happy to look at the wording with you and discuss options.

What Support Can Look Like in Real Life

Busy Mornings

  • Creating a simple visual routine for getting ready
  • Practising one step at a time (e.g. getting dressed, brushing teeth)
  • Supporting calm transitions from home to school or kinder
  • Helping reduce conflict and rushing where possible

After School & Evenings

  • Supporting snack, downtime and homework routines
  • Building in movement or sensory breaks as recommended
  • Creating calmer pre-bed routines and screen-time boundaries
  • Helping siblings feel included and supported

Appointments & Transitions

  • Attending appointments with you (where appropriate & funded)
  • Helping prepare your child for new environments or transitions
  • Supporting the practical side of therapy recommendations at home
  • Helping you organise information for NDIS reviews

Family & Early Support – Frequently Asked Questions

Are you a therapy service?

No. Able Aide is not a clinical therapy service. We are a disability support provider. We work alongside your child’s therapists (for example OT, speech or psychology) to support the practical, everyday side of using their recommendations at home and in the community.

Do you replace my child’s therapists?

No. We do not replace therapists. Instead, we complement their work by helping families and support workers use agreed strategies in real-life routines, where this is appropriate and within our scope.

Can you help if my child has behaviours of concern?

We can support families to follow behaviour support plans and strategies developed by qualified practitioners, within our scope. For complex or high-risk behaviour, specialist behaviour support and clinical input is essential. We are happy to be part of a larger support team.

Do you work with very young children?

Yes. We support families with children in early childhood, including those accessing early childhood NDIS pathways. Our focus is on everyday routines, play, participation and family wellbeing.

Can you support siblings too?

While the NDIS funding is for the participant, we understand that siblings are part of the family system. We are mindful of their needs, and consider them when planning routines and supports at home.

Do you work with schools or kinders?

With your consent, we can communicate with schools, kinders or childcare services where this is helpful and appropriate, especially around routines, transitions and consistent strategies.

Is this support available after school hours?

Where capacity allows, yes. Many families prefer after-school or early evening supports. We will discuss availability and what’s realistic at the time of enquiry.

Can you help us prepare for our child’s NDIS review?

Yes. We can help you reflect on what has changed, gather relevant information, and prepare clear examples of how supports have helped your child and family.

How is this funded in the NDIS plan?

It will depend on your child’s goals and plan wording. Supports may sit under Core or Capacity Building. We can look at the plan with you and discuss options in plain language.

How do we get started?

You can contact us directly, ask your Support Coordinator to refer, or speak with a therapist or provider who can connect you with us. We’ll then talk through your situation, funding and what support might look like.

Need Gentle, Practical Support for Your Family?

If you’re feeling tired, overwhelmed or unsure where to start, you’re not alone. We’re happy to begin with a calm, no-pressure conversation about your child, your family and what you’d like to see change.
