Supporting a Family With Early Childhood Routines

How Able Aide helped a family create calmer mornings, smoother transitions and stronger communication for their young child.

Names and identifying details changed for privacy.

Talk to Able Aide Back to Learn
Family & early support Book a free chat

Overview

"Sara", a 5-year-old NDIS participant, struggled with morning routines, emotional regulation and transitioning to kinder. Her parents felt overwhelmed by mixed therapy advice and inconsistent routines across home and kinder.

Challenges

  • Frequent meltdowns during morning routines
  • Difficulty transitioning between home, car and kinder
  • Limited communication and shared attention
  • Parents overwhelmed by mixed therapy advice
  • Routines inconsistent across home and kinder

What Able Aide did

Visual routine charts

Predictable, step-by-step visuals for mornings and evenings — consistent across home and kinder.

Gentle transition strategies

Timers, advance warnings and calm phrasing to reduce overwhelm during changes between environments.

Play-based engagement

Strengthened shared attention and improved early communication skills through structured play.

Outcomes

  • Calmer mornings and better participation at kinder
  • Fewer meltdowns during transitions
  • Parents confidently using strategies daily
  • Improved communication between family and therapists
"The support worker made our home calmer. We now have a routine that actually works."
