NDIS Support Coordination

The NDIS can feel complicated — paperwork, acronyms, providers, phone calls and portals. Support Coordination means having someone in your corner to make sense of your plan, connect you with the right supports and help problem-solve along the way.

Able Aide provides calm, experienced Support Coordination for NDIS participants and families across Victoria.

Someone in your corner, not just on your file

We listen to your story first — not just your plan. Then we help turn funding and goals into real-world supports that make daily life easier.

What Is NDIS Support Coordination?

Support Coordination is a funded NDIS service that helps you understand, organise and use your plan. Instead of coordinating everything alone, you have someone who can explain your funding, connect you with services, and support you to build your own capacity over time.

We see Support Coordination as a partnership. You remain in control of your choices; we bring clarity, organisation and advocacy to help your plan work for you — not the other way around.

How Able Aide Support Coordination Helps

Understanding Your Plan

We make your NDIS plan easier to understand and use.

  • Explaining funding categories in plain language
  • Clarifying what can and cannot be funded
  • Linking goals to practical supports and services
  • Helping you prioritise what to focus on first

Connecting With Providers

We help you find and engage the right mix of supports.

  • Support workers and in-home supports
  • Allied health providers (OT, physio, speech, psychology)
  • Plan managers and other NDIS supports
  • Community, social and skill-building programs

Ongoing Coordination & Problem-Solving

We stay alongside you as life and needs change.

  • Checking how supports are working for you
  • Supporting with provider issues or changes
  • Helping you prepare for reviews and reassessments
  • Working with your therapists, school or health team

Our Approach to Support Coordination

Every participant, family and situation is different. Our approach is grounded in empathy, realistic expectations and honest communication.

1

We Listen to Your Story

We begin by understanding what life looks like for you — your strengths, challenges, family situation, culture, preferences and hopes for the future.

2

We Build a Realistic Plan

Together we create a practical support map based on your funding, priorities and energy — what to engage first and how it fits into your routine.

3

We Walk Alongside You

We don’t just “set up” supports and disappear. We check in, adjust when things aren’t working, and support you to direct your own supports over time.

Who Support Coordination Is For

Support Coordination can be especially helpful if:

  • You feel overwhelmed by NDIS language and processes
  • You have multiple providers or complex supports
  • Your situation has recently changed (health, housing, family)
  • You’re newly approved and don’t know where to start
  • You’re preparing for a plan reassessment or review
  • You want someone independent to help coordinate everything

Funding & Locations

  • NDIS plan includes Support Coordination (Level 2 or similar)
  • Self-managed or plan-managed participants
  • Participants across Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton & regional Victoria

If you’re unsure whether your plan includes Support Coordination funding, we can help you check and explore options.

Supporting Participants With Complex Needs

Many people we work with experience multiple challenges at once — health needs, mental health, communication differences, trauma history, housing pressures or family stress.

In these situations, Support Coordination can make a big difference. With your consent, we can liaise with your broader support team such as therapists, GPs, schools, hospitals, housing services and community organisations to keep information flowing and supports focused on your priorities.

Working With Families & Carers

With your consent, we involve family or key carers in planning, communication and review, recognising the important role they play.

Respecting Culture, Faith & Identity

Your culture, language, faith and identity shape what “good support” means. We respect this in planning and provider selection wherever possible.

Calm, Honest Conversations

We are honest about capacity, waitlists and service options — while still advocating for what you need. Communication is clear, kind and straightforward.

Preparing for NDIS Plan Reviews

Plan reassessments (often called “reviews”) can be stressful. We help you feel more prepared and heard throughout the process.

Looking Back

  • Reviewing reports and feedback from providers

Looking Forward

  • Gathering supporting information where needed

During & After the Meeting

  • Clarifying decisions and next steps afterwards

Support Coordination – Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have Support Coordination in my plan?

Not every plan includes it. It is usually listed as a separate line item under Capacity Building funding. If you’re unsure, we can help you read your plan or check with the NDIS or your plan manager.

What’s the difference between Support Coordination and Plan Management?

Plan Management focuses on paying invoices and managing your NDIS budget. Support Coordination focuses on helping you understand and use your plan, connect with services and coordinate supports. Many participants have both.

Can you choose providers for me?

We can suggest options, share information and help you compare providers, but the final choice is always yours.

Do you attend appointments or meetings with me?

Where appropriate and funded, we can attend meetings (for example, plan reassessments or provider meetings) to support you and help explain things.

Can you help if I’m unhappy with a provider?

Yes. We can support you to raise concerns, explore alternatives and transition to other providers if needed.

Do you provide Specialist Support Coordination (Level 3)?

Our focus is on Level 2 / standard Support Coordination. If you require Level 3 due to very complex or high-risk circumstances, we can discuss whether we’re the right fit or help you look at other options.

Can Support Coordination be provided remotely?

Yes — many aspects can be done via phone, email or video, especially for participants in regional areas.

How often will I hear from my Support Coordinator?

This depends on your plan, needs and preferences. Some people prefer frequent contact, others only occasional check-ins. We decide this together.

Can you work with my existing support team?

Yes — with your consent, we can liaise with therapists, GP, school, support workers and other services to keep everyone aligned around your goals.

How do I start with Able Aide?

You can contact us directly, speak with your plan manager or ask a provider to send us a referral. We’ll confirm funding, capacity and next steps.

Need Calm, Clear Support with Your NDIS Plan?

If you’re feeling overwhelmed or unsure how to make your plan work for you, we’re happy to start with a simple, no-pressure conversation.
