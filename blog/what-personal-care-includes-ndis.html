What Personal Care Includes Under the NDIS

Personal care is one of the most important supports in an NDIS plan — helping people live safely, comfortably and with greater independence.

This guide explains what personal care covers, who it’s for, and how Able Aide provides it with dignity, consistency and respect.

What does “personal care” mean?

Personal care includes support with everyday tasks that help you look after your body, health and hygiene. The NDIS can fund personal care when disability makes these tasks hard to do safely or independently.

The goal is simple: support your safety, wellbeing and routines — in a way that respects your preferences and dignity.

What personal care can include

✔ Showering & bathing

  • Washing, hygiene and grooming support
  • Safe bathroom routines
  • Building independence where possible

✔ Dressing & grooming

  • Choosing suitable clothing
  • Dressing and undressing
  • Hair care, shaving and grooming

✔ Toileting & continence support

  • Safe toileting support
  • Continence care (as required)
  • Maintaining hygiene and dignity

✔ Mealtime support

  • Preparing simple meals and drinks
  • Assistance to eat (if appropriate)
  • Following swallowing / texture plans where provided

✔ Mobility & transfers

  • Support moving safely around the home
  • Transfers (bed/chair/toilet) where needed
  • Using mobility aids

✔ Medication prompts (non-clinical)

Support workers can provide reminders/prompts as agreed in your plan. Clinical administration requires appropriate training and scope.

✔ Routines & capacity building

  • Morning and evening routines
  • Practising daily living skills
  • Building confidence gradually

Who is personal care for?

Personal care may suit NDIS participants who:

  • Have physical disabilities or reduced mobility
  • Have intellectual or cognitive disabilities
  • Need support due to psychosocial disability or mental health challenges
  • Have neurological or sensory conditions
  • Are children who need routines and daily living support

Support can be flexible — from short visits to longer blocks, mornings or evenings, and in some cases overnight support.

Where personal care fits in your NDIS plan

  • Core Supports → Assistance with Daily Life (common for personal care)
  • Capacity Building → Improved Daily Living (often used for therapy, but may support skill-building around routines)

If you’re unsure which budget applies, a Support Coordinator can help check your plan and explain it in plain language.

How Able Aide provides personal care

Our approach is built on warmth, dignity and consistency. We aim to:

  • Respect your privacy, culture and routine
  • Move at your pace — never rushing
  • Follow all support and safety plans
  • Encourage independence where possible
  • Provide stable, consistent workers wherever possible
