What is Plan Management?

Plan Management explained in plain language

What a Plan Manager does

A Plan Manager sits between you and your NDIS providers — handling the financial side of your plan so you don't have to. They receive invoices from your providers, check them against your plan, pay them on your behalf, and send you regular updates so you always know how your funding is tracking.

You keep full choice and control over who you use and what supports you get. Your Plan Manager just takes care of the admin.

Plan Management vs self-managed

Self-managed: You pay providers directly and claim reimbursement from the NDIA yourself. Full control, but all the admin is on you.

Plan-managed: We pay providers on your behalf and track your budgets. You still choose your providers — including non-registered ones — but we handle all invoicing, payments and reporting.

Plan Management is funded separately in your plan — it comes out of its own funding line, not your Core budget.

What we provide

What Able Aide Plan Management includes

Invoice processing

We receive, verify and process invoices from your providers — checking they match your plan funding and NDIS price limits before paying.

Budget tracking

Clear visibility of your spending across all NDIS support categories — so you always know what's been used and what remains.

Spending summaries

Regular, easy-to-read budget statements so you and your Support Coordinator can see at a glance how your plan is tracking.

Provider communication

We liaise directly with providers to resolve invoice issues, fix errors and ensure payments are processed without delays.

Plan utilisation guidance

We flag when budgets are running low or underspent — giving you time to adjust before your plan period ends.

Non-registered provider payments

Plan Management lets you use non-registered providers (where NDIS rules allow) — giving you more options than agency-managed funding.

Getting started

How to start or switch to Able Aide Plan Management

Simple, low-friction and guided every step of the way.

  1. 01

    Email or call us

    Email planmanagement@ableaide.com.au with your name, phone number, city/state and NDIS number (if you have it). We respond the same business day.

  2. 02

    We confirm your plan details

    We check your plan includes Plan Management funding and confirm your support categories, budgets and key contacts — including your Support Coordinator if you have one.

  3. 03

    Service Agreement & onboarding

    We send a clear, plain-English Service Agreement. Once signed, we register with the NDIA as your Plan Manager and set up your account — usually within 1–2 business days.

  4. 04

    Providers start sending invoices to us

    We notify your providers of the change and give them our invoicing email. From here, invoices come to us — you stop receiving them.
