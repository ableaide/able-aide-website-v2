What Does Support Coordination Include?

Support coordination is about making your NDIS plan more usable in real life. We focus on helping you understand your options, make informed decisions and feel supported – not alone – in the process.

Understanding your plan

We go through your NDIS plan with you in plain, easy-to-follow language. We explain funding categories, what each budget can be used for, and how your goals link to supports.

Finding the right providers

Based on your needs, culture, language, location and preferences, we help identify service options – such as support workers, therapists, community programs and capacity-building supports.

Connecting your supports

Once you’ve chosen providers, we support with introductions, referrals, service agreements, and coordinating start dates so things don’t fall through the gaps.

Managing changes & issues

If something isn’t working – a provider isn’t the right fit, services keep cancelling, or your needs change – we help problem-solve and explore other options.

Preparing for reviews

We help you prepare for plan reviews by gathering reports, reflecting on what worked well, and identifying changes or additional supports you may need.

Building your capacity

Over time, support coordination aims to build your confidence and skills to manage parts of your plan more independently if that’s your goal.

How Support Coordination Works with Able Aide

1

Initial conversation

We learn about your situation, funding, current supports and what has or hasn’t worked in the past. We also discuss your communication preferences and priorities.

2

Plan mapping

We go through your NDIS plan, looking at each budget, your goals and any conditions or recommendations. Together, we design a practical plan to put your funding into action.

3

Ongoing coordination

We support you over time – checking in, helping you respond to changes, communicating with providers, and making sure the plan is still working for you.

Who Is Support Coordination For?

Support coordination is particularly helpful for people who have multiple providers, complex needs, or who feel unsure about where to start with their NDIS plan.

  • Participants with new NDIS plans who feel overwhelmed
  • People with multiple therapy, medical or community supports
  • Families who need help managing services and communication
  • Participants wanting to change or review providers
  • People preparing for plan reviews or major life changes

We can work with you directly, or in collaboration with your family, carers, therapists, doctors, school staff or other important people in your life— always with your consent and preferences at the centre.
