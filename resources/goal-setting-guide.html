NDIS Goal Setting Guide

Clear, practical goal ideas to help your plan match your real life and priorities.

Why NDIS goals matter

Your goals guide what supports are considered reasonable and necessary. The strongest goals reflect real daily life and wellbeing.

What makes a strong NDIS goal

  • Meaningful: Reflects what the participant truly wants
  • Practical: Connected to daily life and participation
  • Flexible: Not locked to one provider or therapy
  • Linked to impact: Explains why support is needed

Goal examples (adapt to suit your situation)

Daily living

  • “I want to build confidence in managing my daily routine and personal care.”
  • “I want support to maintain my home safely and independently.”

Community participation

  • “I want to increase my confidence accessing community activities.”
  • “I want to develop social connections that suit my communication style.”

Wellbeing & safety

  • “I want structured supports to improve emotional regulation and wellbeing.”
  • “I want a consistent team to help reduce stress and unexpected changes.”

Link goals to evidence

Goals are stronger when linked to real examples. Use frequency, safety concerns and impact. See our Evidence & Reports Checklist.
