Our story

Why Able Aide exists

Able Aide was founded to bring consistency, clarity and dignity back to disability support.

Our founder, Syeda Zahra, spent more than a decade working directly with adults, children and families across Melbourne — and saw how the smallest things changed how safe a person felt. Arriving on time. Respecting culture. Communicating clearly. Following through.

She created Able Aide to deliver professional support that is calm, reliable and genuinely person-centred — backed by strong systems, clear communication and a team that listens first.

Today, Able Aide provides Support Coordination and core supports across Melbourne and regional Victoria, with Plan Management available Australia-wide.

From our director

A message from Syeda

Thank you for taking the time to learn about Able Aide.

Over the years I've worked with participants from all walks of life — different backgrounds, different languages, different needs. What I've learned is that good support isn't just about what you do. It's about how you show up, how you listen and how you make someone feel.

Able Aide was built around that understanding. We invest in getting to know each participant — their routines, their culture, their communication style, what a good day looks like for them — before any support begins. That care shapes everything.

I'm proud of the trust that families and coordinators place in us, and I'm committed to ensuring that every person we support feels respected, heard and genuinely cared for.

Warmly,
Syeda Zahra
Founder & Director, Able Aide

What guides us

Mission, vision & values

Our mission

To provide calm, consistent and culturally safe supports — delivered through clear systems and respectful, person-centred care.

Our vision

A community where people with disability feel respected, supported and genuinely in control of their choices and their lives.

Respect

We create environments where every person feels safe, heard and treated with dignity — always.

Reliability

We show up when we say we will, follow through on commitments and communicate clearly when things change.

Collaboration

We work alongside participants, families, coordinators and allied health teams — not in isolation.

Learning

We continuously improve our practice, listen to feedback and stay current with NDIS changes and best practice.

Advocacy

We help participants understand their rights, speak up confidently and get the most from their NDIS plan.

Safety

The physical and emotional safety of every participant is non-negotiable in everything we do.

Our experience

Experience you can trust

Syeda and the Able Aide team bring hands-on experience across a wide range of disability and community settings.

Support coordination

Plan reviews, provider engagement, crisis coordination and NDIS system navigation for participants and families.

Core supports & daily living

CALD & faith communities
