How the NDIS works in regional Victoria

The NDIS operates the same way across Australia — participants receive a plan with funding across Core, Capacity Building and Capital categories. What differs regionally is provider availability, wait times and how services are delivered (in-home vs. community-based).

In regional areas, some participants find a smaller pool of local providers, which makes choosing the right fit — and maintaining consistent supports — even more important.

Core supports

Help with daily living, personal care, domestic tasks and community access. Usually the largest budget category.

Capacity Building

Includes Support Coordination, therapy, skill development and employment supports.

Plan Management

Available Australia-wide — including all regional VIC. Able Aide provides Plan Management nationally.

Key regions – what to know

Geelong & Surf Coast

Geelong has a growing NDIS service network. Key providers are concentrated around the CBD and northern suburbs (Corio, Norlane, Lara). Surf Coast participants may find fewer in-home options and need to factor in travel.

Plan Management available. Contact us to check Support Coordination availability.

Ballarat & Central Highlands

Ballarat has a well-established disability sector but demand often exceeds local supply. Participants in Daylesford, Maryborough and Ararat may have fewer choices and longer wait times for specialist supports.

Plan Management available nationally. Support Coordination — contact us to check.

Bendigo & Loddon Campaspe

Bendigo's disability sector has grown significantly. Participants in surrounding towns (Castlemaine, Kyneton, Echuca) may need to combine local and remote options, particularly for allied health.

Plan Management available. Contact us to check Support Coordination coverage.

Shepparton & Goulburn Valley

Shepparton serves as a regional hub for the Goulburn Valley. Culturally diverse families here often find limited options for culturally appropriate workers. Plan Management and remote support coordination can fill significant gaps.

Plan Management available nationally. Contact us for support options.

Plan Management in regional Victoria

Plan Management is one of the most accessible services for regional participants — it's delivered remotely and available Australia-wide. A good Plan Manager handles your provider invoices, budget tracking and NDIS payment claims, reducing the admin load on families.

  • No need to be locally based — 100% remote service
  • Works with both registered and unregistered providers
  • Helps stretch funding by tracking budgets carefully
  • Responsive to regional participants — no metro-only focus

Tips for regional NDIS participants

Start your search early

In smaller regions, waitlists are common. Begin contacting providers before your plan is approved if possible.

Consider remote-delivered services

Plan Management, Support Coordination and some therapeutic supports can be delivered by video or phone — opening up more options.

Use your LAC or Support Coordinator

Local Area Coordinators and Support Coordinators know the regional landscape. Use them to find providers, fill gaps and navigate reviews.

Document transport costs

In regional areas, travel is often part of accessing supports. Make sure this is reflected in your goals and plan budgets.
