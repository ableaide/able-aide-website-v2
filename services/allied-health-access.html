Allied Health Access Support

Navigating therapy appointments and allied health services can be overwhelming. Able Aide supports NDIS participants to connect with therapists, attend appointments, manage communication, and stay on track with therapy goals.

How we support allied health access

We help you coordinate and access the therapy services that matter most, including:

  • Attending therapy appointments with you
  • Scheduling and coordinating therapy sessions
  • Supporting communication with therapists
  • Understanding therapy recommendations
  • Helping implement therapy strategies at home
  • Transport to and from therapy appointments
  • Assistance at medical & specialist appointments
  • Monitoring therapy progress
  • Encouraging independence between sessions

Types of therapy we assist with

  • Occupational Therapy (OT)
  • Physiotherapy
  • Speech Pathology
  • Psychology & counselling
  • Behaviour support
  • Exercise physiology
  • Early intervention therapies

Our role is to support access — not replace qualified therapists — ensuring you get the most out of your therapy services.

Why Allied Health Access Matters

Therapy plays a vital role in building independence, improving daily living skills, supporting emotional wellbeing and helping participants reach their goals.

Our Allied Health Access Support helps participants:

  • Stay engaged with therapy programs
  • Improve communication with therapists
  • Implement strategies at home
  • Reduce missed appointments
  • Build confidence and skills between sessions
  • Improve overall outcomes and progress

We work closely with families, therapists and support coordinators to ensure you have the right supports in place. Our approach is collaborative, warm, respectful and always centred around your goals.

How Allied Health Access Support Works

1

Understand your therapy needs

We talk with you, your family and care team to understand what therapies you’re engaged in and what supports you require.

2

Coordinate appointments

We schedule sessions, arrange transport and help prepare you for your therapy appointments.

3

Support your therapy goals

We assist with communication, reminders, and follow-through so you can get the most out of each session.

Frequently asked questions

Do you provide therapy?

We support your access to therapy by helping with appointments, communication and follow-up, but the therapy itself is delivered by qualified allied health professionals.

Can you attend therapy appointments with me?

Yes — our workers can accompany you to sessions or support you via transport, reminders and planning.

Do you work with my existing therapists?

Absolutely. We collaborate with your therapy team to ensure supports are consistent and helpful.

What areas do you service?

We support participants across all of Victoria — including Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and nearby regional areas.
