Personal Care & Daily Living Support

Personal care is one of the most important forms of support a person can receive. It involves trust, dignity and a level of comfort that takes time to build. At Able Aide, we take that responsibility seriously. Our approach is gentle, respectful and culturally aware, ensuring your preferences, communication needs and routines always come first.

Whether you need support with showering, dressing, grooming, mobility, meal preparation, medication reminders or daily routines, we work at your pace — never rushed, always respectful.

Support that respects your comfort and choices

We understand how personal and sensitive this type of support can be. That’s why we focus on communication, consistency and relationship-building — so every interaction feels calm, predictable and empowering.

Support Designed Around Your Daily Life

Daily living tasks can take a significant amount of energy — physically, emotionally or mentally. For some people, they may require assistance due to mobility challenges, medical conditions, developmental differences, sensory needs, chronic pain, fatigue or recovering from an injury or surgery. Others simply want a reliable, consistent support worker they trust in their home.

Our personal care support meets you where you are. Whether you prefer quiet, step-by-step guidance, need support for safety, want to build your independence, or require full physical assistance, we tailor our approach to match your comfort, ability and preferences. We also understand the importance of privacy and maintaining dignity — especially during intimate tasks like showering or dressing.

What We Can Help With

Personal Hygiene & Care

Gentle, respectful support with hygiene that keeps you safe and comfortable:

  • Showering, bathing or sponge baths
  • Toileting & continence care
  • Oral hygiene support
  • Hair washing, grooming, drying & shaving
  • Skin care & basic pressure area care (within scope)
  • Assistance with applying creams or lotions (non-clinical)

Dressing, Mobility & Transfers

Physical assistance and mobility support that prioritises safety:

  • Dressing & undressing
  • Choosing appropriate clothing for weather or activities
  • Safe manual handling & transfers
  • Using walkers, hoists or mobility aids (within worker scope)
  • Positioning for comfort & posture support
  • Bed mobility & morning/evening routines

Daily Living & Home Support

Assistance with home routines that help create a safe and comfortable environment:

  • Meal preparation & light cooking
  • Light domestic support (dishes, tidy, surfaces, laundry)
  • Linen changes & making beds
  • Medication reminders (non-clinical)
  • Supporting therapy strategies at home
  • Helping organise home routines

Our Approach to Personal Care

We believe exceptional personal care is not defined by the tasks themselves — it’s defined by how they’re delivered. Our focus is on trust, dignity, communication and respecting your individual differences.

1

We Listen First

Before we begin any support, we take the time to understand your preferences: how you like your shower set up, which products you use, which parts of the routine you want to do yourself and what you need help with.

2

We Work At Your Pace

Some mornings are fast. Others need to move slowly. We adjust to how you're feeling and never rush or push beyond your comfort level.

3

We Provide Consistency

Wherever possible, we match you with the same support workers so your routines become familiar and predictable — especially important for participants with sensory needs, autism, anxiety or trauma history.

Who This Service Is For

Personal Care support may be right for you if:

  • You need physical assistance with showering, mobility or hygiene
  • You experience chronic pain or fatigue that makes tasks difficult
  • You live alone and want daily living support
  • You have sensory differences and need a patient approach
  • You are recovering from an injury or surgery
  • You want to build independence with daily tasks
  • Carers need respite or shared-support arrangements

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I choose my own support worker?

Yes. Matching matters. You can request workers based on gender, communication style, language, cultural background or personality.

Can I change workers if I feel uncomfortable?

Absolutely. Your comfort always comes first. If you feel unsure, unsafe or simply prefer someone different, we will make a change without hesitation.

Is personal care funded by the NDIS?

Yes — it is funded as a Core Support under “Assistance with Daily Life.”

Do you support people with complex care needs?

Yes, including participants with limited mobility, intellectual disability, autism, sensory needs, mental health conditions, or challenging routines. Clinical tasks requiring nursing, however, must be delivered by qualified clinicians.

Will workers follow my therapy plan?

Yes — we can follow strategies from your OT, physio or speech therapist to support consistent progress at home.

Are workers trained in manual handling?

Yes — all support workers receive training in safe transfers, positioning and mobility to reduce risk and ensure safety.

Do you support children?

Yes — we provide age-appropriate personal care routines for children and teens, always respecting family values and safety.

Do I need to prepare my home before workers arrive?

No — support is designed to help you manage daily life realistically, not perfectly.

Can you help after hospital discharge?

Yes — we can support personal care transitions from hospital to home, working with your therapists or discharge team.

What if I feel embarrassed about needing help?

Many participants feel this way at first. Our workers are calm, kind and skilled at helping people feel safe and respected during intimate tasks.

Need Respectful Help With Daily Living?

We understand how personal this support can be. If you’d like to talk through options, get clarity about funding or understand how the process works, we’re here to help.
