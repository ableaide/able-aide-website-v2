Building Confidence in the Community

How Able Aide helped a participant overcome anxiety and gradually rebuild confidence to shop independently, attend appointments and reconnect socially.

Names changed for privacy.

Overview

“Amina”, a young adult with psychosocial disability, experienced high anxiety around leaving the home. Even essential tasks like grocery shopping or attending GP appointments felt overwhelming.

Challenges

  • Severe anxiety outside the home
  • Difficulty with crowds and noise
  • Lost confidence after a negative past experience
  • Limited social connections
  • Avoidant behaviour impacting wellbeing

What Able Aide Did

Slow, step-by-step exposure

Short outdoor walks → local shops → appointments.

Predictable routine

Same worker, same day, same time.

Confidence-building

Grocery lists, planning routes, using public transport safely.

Outcomes

  • Regularly shopping independently
  • Attending all medical appointments
  • Joined a local art program
  • Reduced anxiety and increased confidence
“My support worker never rushed me. Now I can go places I avoided for years.”
