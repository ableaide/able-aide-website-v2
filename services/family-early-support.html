Family & Early Childhood Support

Early years come with big emotions, changing routines, school transitions, therapy appointments and new expectations. It can feel overwhelming for parents and caregivers — and you shouldn’t have to navigate it alone.

We provide gentle, practical NDIS support to help your child participate, regulate, communicate and feel confident at home, school and in the community. Our approach is always family-centred, calm and respectful.

Support for your child — and your whole family

When a child needs support, the whole family feels it. We work alongside parents, carers and therapists to create calm, predictable routines and build confidence step-by-step.

Get Family & Early Support

Helping Everyday Life Feel More Manageable

Many families describe feeling pulled in every direction — juggling kinder or school, behaviour, therapy tasks, morning routines, transitions, sensory needs and emotional overwhelm. Our role is to make home life feel calmer and more predictable.

We focus on real-life situations: mornings, meals, after-school routines, transitioning between activities, attending appointments, managing energy levels, and reducing daily stress for children and caregivers.

What We Can Support With

Daily Routines & Transitions

  • Morning & after-school routines
  • Bedtime support & calming strategies
  • Mealtime help (within NDIS scope)
  • Preparing for appointments or outings
  • Transition support between activities
  • Quiet-time & emotional regulation

Early Development & Participation

  • Play-based engagement
  • Pre-communication & early social skills
  • Following simple routines & instructions
  • Using OT or speech strategies at home
  • Confidence-building for kinder/school

Behaviour & Emotional Support

  • Understanding triggers & patterns
  • Calming & sensory strategies (as advised)
  • Supporting emotional regulation
  • Simple visual systems & routines
  • Following behaviour support plans

Our Family-Centred Approach

Families are the experts in their children. Our role is to listen, understand and offer practical strategies that fit your values, culture, routines and goals.

Listening First

We start by understanding what life looks like for your family — what’s working, what’s difficult and what you’d like to change.

Working Alongside You

We help break therapy recommendations into realistic, achievable steps that fit naturally into your routines.

Respecting Culture & Values

Every family’s culture, language, faith and parenting style is respected. We adapt support so it feels culturally safe and aligned with your home.

Who This Service Is For

Family & Early Support may be ideal if:

  • Your child has an early childhood or school-aged NDIS plan
  • You feel overwhelmed by routines or behaviour
  • You are managing multiple therapies or appointments
  • You need help turning therapy plans into daily routines
  • Transitions (home → school → activities) are difficult
  • Your child needs support building confidence & independence

Family & Early Support – FAQs

Are you a therapy service?

No. We complement your therapists by helping with daily routines, not clinical intervention.

Do you replace therapists?

No — you keep your therapy team. We support the day-to-day side of therapy recommendations.

Do you help with behaviours of concern?

We follow behaviour plans from approved practitioners where appropriate. Complex needs require specialist services.

Do you support very young children?

Yes — including children in the early childhood pathway.

Do you support siblings?

Yes — while NDIS funds the participant, we consider siblings when designing routines.

Do you work with schools/kinders?

Yes — with parent consent, we support communication and consistent routines.

Is after-school support available?

Where capacity allows — many families prefer late afternoon visits.

Can you help prepare for NDIS reviews?

Yes — we help gather information and reflect on what's working.

How is this funded?

Typically under Core or Capacity Building. We can help interpret your plan.

How do we start?

Contact us directly or ask your Support Coordinator to send a referral.
