What is the NDIS early childhood pathway?

The early childhood pathway ‚Äî sometimes called the early childhood approach ‚Äî is designed to help young children with disability or developmental concerns access support as early as possible.

The focus is on early intervention: providing the right support early in life so children can build confidence, participate and develop everyday skills.

Who is it for?

The pathway is typically for:

  • Children under around 9 years of age
  • Children with diagnosed disability
  • Children with developmental delay or emerging concerns
  • Families who feel something isn‚Äôt quite right and want support

Some children receive a full NDIS plan, while others access short-term supports through early childhood partners.

1. Noticing concerns

2. Talking to professionals

Families often begin by speaking with a GP, paediatrician, maternal & child health nurse, kinder or school. They may recommend monitoring, assessments or referral to an early childhood partner.

3. Contacting the early childhood team

The NDIS or your local early childhood partner may ask about:

  • Your child's development and behaviour
  • How they participate at home, kinder or school
  • Any assessments or reports you already have

4. Assessment & eligibility

The NDIA will consider whether your child may benefit from:
