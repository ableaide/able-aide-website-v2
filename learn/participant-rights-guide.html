NDIS Participant Rights Guide

Understanding choice, control, respect, privacy and what to do if something doesn't feel right.

Ask a question Back to Resources

Key participant rights (plain language)

Respect and dignity

All supports should be safe, respectful and treat the participant as an individual.

Choice and control

You should have a say in who supports you, how and when supports are delivered.

Privacy

Your personal information should be handled carefully and shared only when appropriate.

Safe services

Workers should follow safe practice, maintain clear professional boundaries and follow NDIS Code of Conduct.

Right to complain

You can raise concerns without being treated unfairly. Providers must have a complaints process.

Cultural safety

Supports should be delivered in a culturally respectful way that recognises your background and values.

If you have a concern

Step 1 — Raise it early

Share concerns in writing if possible and ask for a clear response within a reasonable timeframe.

Step 2 — Ask for changes

You can request a different worker, a schedule change, or an updated support arrangement.

Step 3 — Use formal pathways

If unresolved, use your provider's complaints pathway, contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission, or seek advocacy support.

Advocacy support in Victoria

Advocacy organisations can provide free, confidential support to help you understand and exercise your rights.
