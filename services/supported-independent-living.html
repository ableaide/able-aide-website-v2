What is SIL?

Understanding Supported Independent Living

SIL is one of the most significant NDIS supports available. Here's what it means in plain language.

What SIL is

Supported Independent Living is NDIS-funded daily support that helps you live as independently as possible in your own home — whether that's a private home, shared house, or specialist accommodation. Support workers assist with daily tasks around your routines and goals.

SIL is funded under Core Supports in your NDIS plan, under the "Assistance with Daily Life" support category.

SIL vs SDA — what's the difference?

SIL is the support — the workers who help you day-to-day. SDA (Specialist Disability Accommodation) is the housing itself — a specially designed or modified home.

Many participants need both SIL and SDA. They are separate funding categories in your NDIS plan and can be provided by different organisations. Learn more about SDA →

What we provide

What Able Aide SIL support includes

Every participant is different. Our SIL support is built around you — not a one-size roster.

Personal care

Showering, grooming, dressing, oral hygiene and continence support — provided with dignity and respect, always at your pace.

Meals & nutrition

Assistance with meal preparation, cooking, grocery planning and maintaining a healthy, culturally appropriate diet.

Household tasks

Cleaning, laundry, tidying and home maintenance assistance to keep your living space comfortable and safe.

Morning & evening routines

Consistent support to start and end the day well — tailored to your preferred timing, communication style and routine.

Medication assistance

Prompting and assistance with medications as part of your daily routine, in line with your health plan and provider guidance.

Community access & appointments

Support to attend appointments, activities, social programs and community events as part of a balanced, connected life.

Overnight & active night support

Sleepover and active overnight support for participants who need assistance or supervision during the night.

Building independence

Support that gradually builds your skills and confidence — so you can do more on your own over time, at your pace.

Shared living support

Support in shared accommodation settings — balancing house routines with each participant's individual needs and preferences.

How it works

How we set up SIL with you

We take time before support begins — because getting it right from the start matters.

  1. 01

    Initial conversation

    We talk through your situation, current living arrangement, support needs and what a good day looks like for you. No pressure, no forms — just a real conversation.

  2. 02

    We learn your story

    Before any worker walks through your door, we learn your communication style, routines, culture, preferences and goals. This shapes everything — who we match you with and how support is delivered.

  3. 03

    Worker matching

    We carefully match you with workers who fit your needs — not just whoever is available. Shared language, cultural background, personality and schedule all factor in.

  4. 04

    Support begins — gently

    We start at your pace, with regular check-ins. Rosters and supports are adjusted as you settle in and as your needs evolve over time.

Who SIL is for

Is SIL right for you?

SIL may be a good fit if you recognise yourself in any of the following.

You need regular daily support

You require ongoing assistance with daily living tasks — personal care, meals, household tasks — that goes beyond what family or informal supports can provide.

You want to live more independently

You want to live in your own home or move out of family care, and need the right support structure to make that possible safely and confidently.

You're moving into shared living

You're transitioning into or already living in a shared house and need support workers who can balance individual needs within a shared environment.

Your support needs are changing

Your needs have increased and your current informal or drop-in supports are no longer enough to keep you safe and well at home.

You want culturally safe support

Your culture, language, faith and family values matter deeply to you — and you need workers who understand and respect that in your home environment.

You're new to the NDIS

You're navigating the NDIS for the first time and need guidance on whether SIL funding is right for you and how to get it included in your plan.

NDIS funding

How to get SIL in your NDIS plan

SIL requires specific funding and evidence. Here's what to know.

What evidence is needed?

To get SIL funded in your plan, the NDIA typically requires evidence of your daily support needs — including an OT assessment, a functional capacity assessment, and a SIL Roster of Care showing what hours of support you need and why.

Your Support Coordinator can help coordinate this evidence. If you don't yet have a Support Coordinator, we can help connect you with one.

How Able Aide can help

We can work with you and your Support Coordinator to:

  • Understand whether SIL is the right fit
  • Explain what evidence is needed for your plan
  • Prepare a Roster of Care document
  • Transition smoothly once SIL is approved
