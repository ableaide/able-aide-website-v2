Personal Care & Daily Living

We provide gentle, respectful personal care so you can feel safe, comfortable and supported in your own home – without losing your independence or dignity.

Personal care is deeply personal. We move at your pace, follow your preferences and always ask before we assist.

Support that fits your routines

Together, we design a personal care plan that respects your routines, culture, gender preferences and comfort level – not a one-size-fits-all roster.

  • Support with showering, dressing and grooming
  • Help with meals, mobility and daily tasks
  • Workers matched to your personality and needs
Talk about personal care

What We Can Help With

Personal care looks different for everyone. Some people need a little support to get started in the morning; others need full assistance throughout the day. We tailor supports to your abilities, strengths and goals.

Hygiene & grooming

We can support with showering, bathing, washing hair, toileting and continence care, always focusing on privacy and consent. Supports can be hands-on or prompting only, depending on your needs and comfort.

Dressing & transfers

Assistance with dressing, undressing, choosing outfits, and safely getting in and out of bed, chairs or wheelchairs using the appropriate equipment and techniques.

Meals & nutrition support

Help with meal preparation, heating food, cutting food, setting up mealtimes and cleaning up afterwards. Where relevant, we can follow allied health or dietitian recommendations about food textures and routines.

Medication prompts

Non-clinical reminders to take medication as prescribed, assisting with routines and organisation. (Clinical medication administration remains with nurses or medical staff.)

Light household tasks

Help with small tasks linked to daily living such as changing bed linen, tidying living areas, or putting on a load of personal laundry as part of your routine.

Daily structure & motivation

Some days are harder than others. We can provide gentle encouragement, pacing and structure to help you get through your routine without feeling rushed or pressured.

How Personal Care Supports Work

1

We listen first

We begin by understanding what you’re comfortable with, any past experiences (good or bad), your cultural or religious needs, and your preferred worker gender and communication style.

2

We plan your routine

Together, we map out what support looks like – days, times, specific tasks, equipment needed, and any “non-negotiables” for your privacy and comfort.

3

We provide consistent support

We do our best to keep your support team consistent so you see familiar faces, not new workers every week. We check in regularly and adjust the plan as your needs change.

Who Is Personal Care For?

Our personal care and daily living supports are available to NDIS participants of different ages, abilities and support needs across Victoria.

  • People who need help with showering, dressing or toileting
  • Participants with mobility or balance challenges
  • People living with chronic illness or disability
  • Older participants needing extra support to remain at home
  • Families who need reliable support to manage daily routines

We can work alongside family members, carers, support coordinators and allied health professionals to ensure support is coordinated and safe. Our aim is always to promote dignity, choice and control – never to rush, pressure or talk over the person we’re supporting.
