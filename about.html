About Able Aide

Able Aide was founded to provide warm, reliable and person-centred disability support for NDIS participants and families across Victoria.

We believe that the right support can make daily life feel calmer, safer and more hopeful – not more complicated.

Your journey, our support

Behind every plan, there is a person, a family and a story. We take time to understand all three before we talk about rosters or tasks.

Talk to Able Aide

Our Story

Able Aide was created after years of seeing families and participants feel overwhelmed by the system, let down by inconsistent support, or unheard when trying to explain what they really needed.

Our founder, Syeda, has over a decade of experience in disability support, support coordination, advocacy and direct care. Through this work, she saw firsthand that:

  • Genuine listening
  • Clear communication
  • Consistency of workers
  • Respect for culture and family

can make a profound difference in a person's confidence, wellbeing and sense of safety. Able Aide grew from a desire to build a service where these values are not “nice-to-have” but essential.

What matters to us

  • Listening first, then suggesting supports
  • Respecting your routines, culture and boundaries
  • Doing what we say we will do
  • Being honest about our capacity
  • Working with you, not just for you

Our Values in Practice

Respect

We respect your home, your culture, your family, your faith, your pronouns and your choices. We don’t make assumptions — we ask before we assist.

Reliability

We know how disruptive last-minute cancellations or staff changes can be. We aim for consistency and honest communication about capacity.

Collaboration

We work alongside you, your family, support coordinator, therapists and other providers. Support works best when everyone works together.

Experience You Can Trust

We bring together hands-on disability support experience, support coordination skills and a strong understanding of the NDIS.

NDIS support coordination

Experience helping participants and families understand plans, coordinate services, problem-solve issues and prepare for reviews.

Core supports & daily living

Years of practical experience providing personal care, community access, domestic assistance and family-focused support across Victoria.

Working with diverse communities

We have supported participants from many cultural, language and faith backgrounds, and we always take time to understand what is important to you.

How We Work With You

1

We listen

We take time to understand your goals, routines, culture, concerns and what “good support” looks like for you — not just what is written in the plan.

2

We plan together

We work with you, your family and your coordinator (if you have one) to create a plan that fits your life and preferences.

3

We keep checking in

Your needs may change — your support should too. We check in regularly and adjust where possible.
