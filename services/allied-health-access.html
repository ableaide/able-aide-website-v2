Allied Health Access

We help you connect with the right allied health professionals – such as occupational therapists, physiotherapists and speech pathologists – and support you to follow through with recommendations.

Our role is to make therapy access easier, more organised and aligned with your goals.

Bridging the gap between you and your therapy team

It’s one thing to have funding in your plan. It’s another to actually find providers, get appointments and integrate therapy into real life. We help with that gap.

Discuss allied health access

How We Support Allied Health Access

We are not an allied health provider ourselves. Instead, we help you access and coordinate your allied health services as part of your NDIS plan.

Finding suitable therapists

We help you look for allied health providers that fit your needs, such as OT, physio, speech therapy, behaviour support or psychology—considering location, culture, language, wait times and specialisation.

Referrals & intake

We assist with completing referral forms, sharing relevant information (with your consent) and following up on waiting lists or intake processes.

Appointment support

We can support with booking appointments, reminders, planning travel, and in some cases attending appointments for support and communication.

Following recommendations

Where appropriate, we help build therapist recommendations into your daily support – for example, practicing exercises during personal care, or using communication aids during community outings.

Reports & NDIS reviews

We can help you keep track of reports requested by the NDIS or planners and support the process of collecting these from therapists ahead of plan reviews.

Communication between providers

With your permission, we help support communication between you, your therapists, support workers, support coordinators and other providers so everyone stays on the same page.

What We Do (and Don’t) Do

What we do

  • Help you understand your allied health-related funding
  • Assist with finding and contacting allied health providers
  • Support with organising appointments and logistics
  • Integrate therapy strategies into everyday support (where appropriate)
  • Help you prepare for NDIS reviews with relevant reports

What we don’t do

  • We do not provide therapy or clinical services ourselves
  • We do not make clinical decisions or change therapy plans
  • We do not replace your therapists, doctors or other health professionals

All clinical recommendations and assessments remain the responsibility of your qualified allied health providers and medical professionals.
