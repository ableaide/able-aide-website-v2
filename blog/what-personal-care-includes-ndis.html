What Personal Care Includes Under the NDIS

Personal care is one of the most important supports in an NDIS plan — helping people live safely, comfortably and with greater independence at home and in their community.

This guide explains exactly what personal care covers, who it’s for, and how Able Aide provides it with dignity, consistency and respect.

What does “personal care” mean?

Personal care includes support with daily tasks that help you look after your body, health and hygiene. The NDIS funds personal care when disability makes these tasks difficult to manage independently.

The goal is simple: to support your safety, wellbeing and routines — in a way that respects your preferences, comfort and dignity.

What personal care includes (NDIS approved)

✔ Showering & bathing support

  • Assistance with washing, hygiene and grooming
  • Setting up safe bathroom routines
  • Supporting independence wherever possible

✔ Dressing & grooming

  • Selecting clothing
  • Dressing and undressing
  • Hair brushing, shaving and personal grooming

✔ Toileting & continence support

  • Using the toilet safely
  • Supporting continence needs
  • Maintaining comfort, privacy and hygiene

✔ Mealtime support

  • Assistance preparing simple meals
  • Help with feeding (as appropriate)
  • Following swallowing or texture plans from professionals

✔ Mobility & transfers

  • Getting in/out of bed or chairs
  • Using hoists, slide sheets or mobility aids
  • Moving safely around the home

✔ Medication prompts (non-clinical)

Support workers can remind participants to take medication — but cannot administer clinical doses that require medical training.

✔ Daily routines & capacity-building

  • Morning and evening routines
  • Practising safe, independent living skills
  • Building confidence over time

Who is personal care for?

NDIS personal care supports participants who:

  • Have physical disabilities
  • Have developmental or cognitive disabilities
  • Require assistance due to mental health conditions
  • Have sensory or neurological conditions
  • Are children with early childhood needs

Support is flexible — from 30 minutes to several hours, mornings, evenings or overnight.

How Able Aide provides personal care

Our approach is built on warmth, consistency and respect. Our support workers:

  • Respect your privacy, culture and communication style
  • Move at your pace — never rushing
  • Maintain dignity at all times
  • Follow all support plans, safety guidance and clinical recommendations
  • Encourage independence where possible

Personal care is more than daily tasks — it’s about safety, trust and feeling comfortable with the person supporting you.

How personal care fits into your NDIS plan

Personal care is usually funded under:

  • Core Supports – Daily Activities
  • Capacity Building – Daily Living (for routines, training or skill-building)

If you’re unsure how your funding works, we can help explain it clearly.
