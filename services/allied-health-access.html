What we support

What allied health access can include

We don't replace therapists — we help you access them and stay organised around appointments, reports and communication.

Finding the right providers

Support to identify and contact OTs, physios, psychologists, speech pathologists and other allied health providers who are appropriate for your NDIS goals and accessible in your area.

Referrals & bookings

Assistance with enquiries, intake forms, referral letters and organising appointments — including telehealth options where suitable to reduce travel and waiting time.

Assessments & reports

Help to organise and follow up on functional capacity assessments, OT reports, AT assessments and other reports needed for plan reviews and equipment approvals.

Appointment support

Attending sessions with you (if you choose) — supporting communication, note-taking and helping you understand and remember what was discussed and recommended.

Therapy-informed daily routines

Supporting you to apply therapist recommendations into your daily routines — within scope and always with your consent and your therapist's guidance.

Coordinated team communication

With your consent, we keep families, Support Coordinators, therapists and other providers aligned — reducing miscommunication and ensuring nothing is missed.

Allied health types

Types of allied health we can help you access

Occupational therapy (OT)

Functional capacity assessments, home modifications, assistive technology recommendations, daily living skills programs and sensory assessments.

Speech pathology

Communication assessments, AAC (augmentative and alternative communication) support, swallowing assessments and social communication programs.

Psychology & mental health

Psychological assessments, therapeutic support, behavioural support coordination and mental health capacity assessments for NDIS planning.

Physiotherapy

Mobility assessments, exercise programs, pain management, positioning and postural support — particularly for participants with physical disability.

Behaviour support

Connecting with positive behaviour support practitioners for participants with complex behaviour needs. For direct behaviour support, see Able Aide Health.

Dietetics & nutrition

Accessing dietitians for participants with specific dietary or nutritional needs as part of their NDIS-funded health supports.

How it works

How we support allied health access

You shouldn't feel like the only one chasing referrals, bookings and follow-ups.

  1. 01

    We understand your goals & needs

    We start by understanding what you want support with — whether it's a specific assessment, ongoing therapy, or help navigating a complex referral pathway.

  2. 02

    We identify the right providers

    We help you find allied health providers who are appropriate for your needs, available in your area (or via telehealth), and accessible within your NDIS funding.

  3. 03

    We organise the referrals & bookings

    We handle enquiries, intake forms and booking coordination — reducing the administrative burden on you and your family.

  4. 04

    We keep everything on track

    We follow up on appointments, reports and recommendations — keeping your support team aligned and ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

Who it's for

Who allied health access support helps

Participants needing assessments

For plan reviews, assistive technology, home modifications, equipment or therapy planning — we help you get the right reports organised efficiently.

Families feeling overwhelmed

Managing multiple providers, waitlists and appointments is exhausting. We take on the coordination so families can focus on what matters.

Participants in regional Victoria

Access to allied health can be harder in regional areas. We help identify telehealth options and providers who service your location.

Children & young people

Coordinating OT, speech, psychology and other therapies for children — keeping schools, families and therapy teams on the same page.

Participants with complex needs

Multiple allied health providers, complex referral pathways and specialist assessments — we keep it organised and communicate clearly with everyone involved.

Participants new to therapy

Not sure which type of allied health you need or where to start? We help you understand your options and navigate the referral process clearly.
