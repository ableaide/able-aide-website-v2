Building Confidence in the Community

How Able Aide supported a young adult with psychosocial disability to overcome anxiety, rebuild independence and reconnect with meaningful community participation.

Names and identifying details changed for privacy.

Talk to Able Aide Back to Learn
Community participation Book a free chat

Overview

"Amina", a young adult with a psychosocial disability, experienced severe anxiety when leaving home. Even essential tasks like grocery shopping or attending GP appointments felt overwhelming and unpredictable.

Challenges

  • High anxiety outside the home
  • Difficulty with crowds, noise and unfamiliar environments
  • Loss of confidence after a past negative experience
  • Limited social connections
  • Avoidance behaviours impacting health and wellbeing

What Able Aide did

Gentle, step-by-step exposure

Short walks → local shops → appointments. Moving at Amina's pace with no pressure.

Predictable routine

Same worker, same time each week to reduce uncertainty and build familiarity.

Confidence-building strategies

Planning routes, using public transport safely, practising independence gradually.

Outcomes

  • Shopping independently each week
  • Attending all medical appointments
  • Joined a local art and social program
  • Significantly reduced anxiety and increased confidence
"My support worker never rushed me. Now I can go places I avoided for years."
