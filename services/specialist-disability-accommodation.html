What is SDA?

Understanding Specialist Disability Accommodation

SDA in plain language

Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA) is NDIS-funded housing — a specially designed or modified home built to meet the needs of people with extreme functional impairment or very high support needs.

SDA funding pays for the home itself — the accessible features, design, and building costs. It does not cover the support workers who help you daily. That is Supported Independent Living (SIL), which is funded separately.

Who SDA is designed for

SDA is available to participants who have very high support needs or extreme functional impairment — typically around 6% of NDIS participants. It is not available to all participants with a disability.

To access SDA you need to demonstrate through assessments that standard housing cannot meet your needs, and that SDA would reduce your overall support costs or enable you to live more independently.

Housing types

The four SDA design categories

SDA is built across four design categories, each suited to different types of disability-related needs.

Improved Liveability

Designed for participants with sensory, intellectual or cognitive impairments. Features include better lighting, clear sightlines, reduced sensory clutter, tactile wayfinding and easy-to-understand layout.

Suitable for: participants with autism, sensory processing differences, cognitive or intellectual disability.

Fully Accessible

Designed for participants with significant physical disability who require a wheelchair-accessible home. Features include wider doorways, roll-under benches, accessible bathrooms, ramps and turning spaces.

Suitable for: participants who use a wheelchair or have significant mobility limitations.

Robust

Designed for participants whose behaviour may damage standard housing. Features include reinforced walls, impact-resistant fittings, secure spaces, soundproofing and durable materials throughout.

Suitable for: participants with complex behavioural support needs or psychosocial disability.

High Physical Support

The most specialised SDA category — designed for participants with very high physical support needs. Features include ceiling hoists, hospital-grade bathrooms, home automation, emergency power and space for complex equipment.

Suitable for: participants with acquired brain injury, spinal cord injury or complex physical disability requiring significant daily physical assistance.

SDA + SIL together

How SDA and SIL work together

Most participants in SDA housing also need SIL support. Here's how they relate.

SDA = the home

SDA funding pays for the specialised housing itself — the building, design features, modifications and ongoing structural costs. This is your home.

SIL = the support

SIL funding pays for the support workers who assist you in that home — with daily tasks like personal care, meals, medication and community access.

Able Aide coordinates both

We can provide SIL support within an SDA property — and help you coordinate between your SDA landlord/provider and your SIL supports so everything works together smoothly.

Getting SDA in your plan

How to access SDA funding

The SDA process requires evidence, assessment and coordination. We can help guide you through each step.

  1. 01

    Check your eligibility

    SDA is not available to all NDIS participants. You need to have very high support needs or extreme functional impairment. Talk to us or your Support Coordinator about whether SDA is likely to be appropriate for your situation.

  2. 02

    Gather evidence & assessments

    An OT functional capacity assessment is typically required, along with evidence from other treating practitioners. Your Support Coordinator will coordinate this. We can help connect you with the right assessors.

  3. 03

    NDIA consideration

    The NDIA reviews your evidence and determines whether SDA is reasonable and necessary. If approved, your plan will include an SDA funding amount and a specific design category.

  4. 04

    Find suitable SDA housing

    Once funded, you choose an SDA property from a registered SDA provider that matches your design category. We can help you understand what to look for and connect you with housing options in Melbourne.

  5. 05

    Set up SIL support in your new home

    Once your SDA home is confirmed, Able Aide can provide your SIL support — matched to your needs, your routines and your goals in that home.
