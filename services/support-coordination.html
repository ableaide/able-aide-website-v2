NDIS Support Coordination

The NDIS can feel complicated — lots of paperwork, acronyms, providers and phone calls. Support Coordination is about having someone walk alongside you to make sense of your plan, connect you with the right supports and problem-solve challenges as they arise.

Able Aide provides calm, experienced support coordination for NDIS participants and families across Victoria.

Someone in your corner, not just on your file

We listen to your story first — not just your plan. Then we help you turn funding and goals into real-world supports that make daily life easier.

What Is NDIS Support Coordination?

Support Coordination is a funded NDIS service designed to help you understand, organise and use your plan. Instead of trying to coordinate everything alone, you have someone who can: explain your funding, connect you to services, help things run smoothly and support you to build your own capacity over time.

We see Support Coordination as a partnership. You remain in control of your choices; we bring clarity, organisation and advocacy to help your plan work for you — not the other way around.

How Able Aide Support Coordination Helps

Understanding Your Plan

We help you make sense of what’s written in your plan and how it can work for you:

  • Explaining funding categories in plain language
  • Clarifying what can and cannot be funded
  • Linking goals to practical supports and services
  • Helping you prioritise what to focus on first

Connecting With Providers

We assist you to find and engage the right mix of supports, such as:

  • Support workers and in-home supports
  • Allied health providers (OT, physio, speech, psychology)
  • Plan managers and other NDIS supports
  • Community, social and skill-building programs

Ongoing Coordination & Problem-Solving

We stay alongside you as life and needs change:

  • Checking how supports are working for you
  • Supporting with provider issues or changes
  • Helping you prepare for reviews and reassessments
  • Working with your therapists, school or health team

Our Approach to Support Coordination

Every participant, family and situation is different. Our approach is grounded in empathy, realistic expectations and honest communication.

1

We Listen to Your Story

We begin by understanding what life looks like for you — your strengths, challenges, family situation, culture, preferences and hopes for the future. We don’t jump straight to “services” without hearing your story first.

2

We Build a Realistic Plan Together

We work with you to create a practical support map based on your funding, priorities and energy. This includes which supports to engage first, and how they might fit into your routine.

3

We Walk Alongside You

We don’t just “set up” supports and disappear. We check in regularly, adjust when things aren’t working, and support you to develop skills and confidence to direct your own supports over time.

Who Support Coordination Is For

Support Coordination can be especially helpful if:

  • You feel overwhelmed by the NDIS language and processes
  • You have multiple providers or complex supports
  • Your situation has recently changed (health, housing, family)
  • You’re newly approved for an NDIS plan and don’t know where to start
  • You’re preparing for a plan reassessment or review
  • You want someone independent to help coordinate everything

Funding & Locations

  • NDIS plan includes Support Coordination funding (Level 2 or similar)
  • Self-managed or plan-managed participants
  • Participants across Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton & regional Victoria

If you’re unsure whether your plan includes Support Coordination funding, we can help you check and explore options.

Supporting Participants With Complex Needs

Many of the people we work with experience multiple challenges at once — physical health needs, mental health, communication differences, trauma history, housing instability or family pressures. We understand that life around the NDIS can be messy and complicated.

In these situations, Support Coordination can make a big difference. We liaise with your broader support team (where appropriate), such as therapists, GPs, schools, hospitals, housing services and community organisations, to help keep information flowing and ensure supports stay focused on your priorities.

Working With Families & Carers

With your consent, we involve family members or key carers in planning, communication and review. We recognise the important role they play and make space for their insight and concerns.

Respecting Culture, Faith & Identity

Your cultural background, faith, language and identity shape what “good support” means to you. We respect and incorporate these in provider selection and planning where possible.

Calm, Honest Conversations

We aim to be honest and realistic about capacity, waitlists and service options — without giving up on advocating for what you need. Our communication is clear, kind and straightforward.

Preparing for NDIS Plan Reviews

Plan reassessments (often called “reviews”) can bring up stress and uncertainty. We support you to feel more prepared and heard in that process.

Looking Back

  • Reflecting on what has worked well
  • Identifying gaps or supports that didn’t work
  • Reviewing reports and feedback from providers

Looking Forward

  • Clarifying your goals for the next period
  • Identifying new needs or changes in circumstances
  • Gathering supporting information where needed

During the Meeting

  • Helping you understand what to expect
  • Supporting you to express your needs and goals
  • Clarifying decisions and next steps afterwards

Support Coordination – Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have Support Coordination in my plan?

Not every plan includes Support Coordination. It is usually listed as a separate line item under Capacity Building funding. If you’re unsure, we can help you read your plan or check with the NDIS or your plan manager.

What’s the difference between Support Coordination and Plan Management?

Plan Management focuses on paying invoices and managing your NDIS budget. Support Coordination focuses on helping you understand and use your plan, connect with services and coordinate supports. Many participants have both.

Can you choose providers for me?

We can suggest options, share information and help you compare providers, but the final choice is always yours. Our role is to support informed decision-making, not to decide for you.

Do you attend appointments or meetings with me?

Yes — where appropriate and funded, we can attend meetings with you (for example, plan reassessments or provider meetings) to help explain things and make sure your voice is heard.

Can you help if I’m unhappy with a provider?

Yes. We can support you to raise concerns respectfully, explore alternatives, and transition to other providers if needed, always with your safety and preferences in mind.

Do you provide Specialist Support Coordination (Level 3)?

At this stage, our focus is on Level 2/standard Support Coordination. If you require Specialist Support Coordination due to very complex or high-risk circumstances, we can discuss whether we are the right fit or help you look at other options.

Can Support Coordination be provided remotely?

Yes, some aspects can be done over phone, email or video, especially for participants in regional or remote areas or during health restrictions.

How often will I hear from my Support Coordinator?

That depends on your plan, needs and preferences. Some participants need frequent contact; others only need occasional check-ins. We agree on this together at the start.

Can you work with my existing support team?

Yes — with your permission, we can liaise with your therapists, GP, school, support workers and other services to keep everyone aligned around your goals.

How do I start Support Coordination with Able Aide?

You can contact us directly, speak with your plan manager, or ask your existing Support Coordinator or provider to send us a referral. We’ll then confirm funding, capacity and next steps.

Need Calm, Clear Support With Your NDIS Plan?

If you’re feeling overwhelmed, stuck or unsure how to make your NDIS plan work for you, we’re here to help. We’re happy to start with a simple, no-pressure conversation.
