Terms of Service

These Terms explain how Able Aide provides services, what you can expect from us, and what we ask from participants, families and referrers. We aim to keep things clear, fair and consistent with NDIS quality and safeguarding requirements.

1. About These Terms

These Terms of Service (“Terms”) set out how Able Aide (“we”, “our”, “us”) provides supports and services to NDIS participants and families across Victoria, and how we use our website.

By engaging our services, making a referral, or continuing to receive supports from us, you agree to these Terms. If you do not agree, please contact us so we can discuss your concerns and, if needed, help you find alternative options.

2. Who These Terms Apply To

These Terms apply to:

  • NDIS participants who receive supports from Able Aide
  • Parents, guardians and informal carers engaged in decision-making
  • Support coordinators, plan managers and referrers
  • Website visitors using our information and forms

3. Our Services

We provide disability supports that may include personal care, daily living assistance, community access, support coordination, allied health access support and family/early support, depending on our current capacity and your NDIS plan.

Details of your specific supports, days, times and rates will be confirmed with you in writing (for example, through a Service Agreement, support coordination schedule or written communication).

We do not provide medical, clinical or emergency services. In an emergency, always call 000.

4. Funding, Fees & Invoicing

  • Our fees are generally in line with current NDIS Pricing Arrangements (where applicable).
  • We will explain the hourly rate, travel, report-writing or other charges before services begin.
  • Funding type (self-managed, plan-managed or other) will be confirmed at intake.
  • Invoices are issued to the NDIS participant, plan manager or other agreed party.
  • Payment timeframes will be outlined in your Service Agreement or written communication.

If NDIS or funding rules change, we may need to update our rates. We will provide reasonable notice of any changes and discuss them with you.

5. Participant, Family & Referrer Responsibilities

We ask that you, your family and your support team:

  • Provide accurate information about your needs, supports, risks and preferences
  • Let us know about changes to your address, contact details or funding
  • Inform us if your circumstances, health or risk factors change
  • Treat our staff with respect and follow the NDIS Code of Conduct
  • Give reasonable notice for cancellations (see section 6)
  • Use our services in a lawful and reasonable way

6. Cancellations, Changes & Ending Supports

6.1 Cancellations

We understand that things change. Wherever possible, please provide us with reasonable notice (for example, at least 2 business days) if you need to cancel or change a booking.

In some cases, and where consistent with NDIS rules, short-notice or same-day cancellations may be billed in line with current NDIS Pricing Arrangements. We will explain this clearly in your Service Agreement or at intake.

6.2 Ending or Pausing Supports

You may request to end or pause services at any time. We encourage a conversation so we can:

  • Understand your reasons and address concerns where possible
  • Plan a safe transition to other providers if needed
  • Finalise outstanding appointments, reports or invoices

Able Aide may also need to end services where:

  • There are ongoing safety concerns that cannot be adequately managed
  • Funding is exhausted or withdrawn
  • There is repeated non-payment of fees
  • There is persistent behaviour that breaches the NDIS Code of Conduct or places staff at risk

We will provide reasonable notice where possible and, where appropriate, help you explore other options.

7. Safety, Risk & Incident Management

Safety is a shared responsibility. We follow NDIS quality and safeguarding requirements, and we may need to record and report certain incidents (for example, injuries, serious behaviours of concern or allegations of abuse).

We ask you to:

  • Provide accurate information about known risks or behaviours
  • Maintain a safe environment during in-home supports
  • Inform us promptly if a safety situation changes

In an emergency, always call 000 first. Our services are not a crisis or emergency response.

8. Privacy & Confidentiality

We respect your privacy and follow Australian privacy law and NDIS Practice Standards when handling your personal information.

Our Privacy Policy explains in detail:

  • What information we collect
  • How we use and store it
  • Who we might share it with and why
  • Your rights to access and correct your information

9. Website Use, Content & Links

Information on our website is general in nature and is not a substitute for medical, legal, financial or clinical advice. We aim to keep content accurate and up to date, but we cannot guarantee it is always complete or free from error.

Our website may contain links to other websites. We do not control and are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of external sites.

10. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Able Aide is not liable for any loss or damage you experience as a result of:

  • Using or relying on general information on our website
  • Matters outside our reasonable control (for example, sudden illness, emergencies or system outages)
  • Services provided by other organisations we refer you to

Nothing in these Terms limits rights you may have under Australian Consumer Law or your rights as an NDIS participant.

11. Feedback & Complaints

We welcome feedback — positive, neutral or negative. It helps us improve and keep people safe.

You can provide feedback or make a complaint by:

  • Speaking with us directly
  • Emailing enquiries@ableaide.com.au
  • Calling 0435 039 110
  • Using our online contact form

You also have the right to complain directly to the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission or to use an independent advocate. We can help you find advocacy services if you wish.

12. Changes to These Terms

We may update these Terms to reflect changes in law, NDIS requirements, our services or how we operate. The latest version will always be available on our website.

Where changes are significant and affect participants receiving ongoing supports, we will take reasonable steps to let you know and discuss any impact.

13. Governing Law

These Terms are governed by the laws of Victoria, Australia. Any disputes will be dealt with under Victorian jurisdiction.
