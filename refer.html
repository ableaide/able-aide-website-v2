Refer an NDIS Participant

Support coordinators, plan managers, allied health professionals, schools, community workers and families can use this form to refer an NDIS participant to Able Aide.

We respond promptly with clear communication, realistic expectations about capacity, and a person-centred intake process.

Current capacity: Core Supports (personal care, community access), Support Coordination, Family & Early Support – across Greater Melbourne and key regional centres.

Who can refer?

  • Support Coordinators & Specialist Support Coordinators
  • Plan Managers & LACs
  • Therapists & allied health providers
  • Schools & community workers
  • Parents, guardians & carers
  • Participants (self-referral)

If you’re unsure whether Able Aide is the right fit, feel free to contact us first for a brief discussion.

What Happens Next?

1

We review the referral

We carefully review supports requested, location, risk notes, communication needs and funding type to determine suitability and capacity.

2

We confirm capacity

If we can proceed, we contact the referrer and/or participant promptly. If we are at capacity or not the right fit, we communicate this honestly and respectfully.

3

We begin intake

We gather any additional details, discuss goals and preferences, and start working together on a safe, person-centred support plan.
