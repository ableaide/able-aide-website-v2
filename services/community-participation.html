Community Access & Social Participation

Being part of the community is essential for wellbeing. Whether it’s attending appointments, going shopping, joining activities, seeing friends, or simply spending time outdoors, community access helps build confidence, independence and connection.

We support participants across Victoria with safe, flexible and personalised community access at your pace.

Support that helps you feel confident

We understand that going out can feel overwhelming. Our support workers walk alongside you — encouraging confidence while respecting your pace and comfort.

Talk about community access

Support That Helps You Live Life Fully

Community access means more than “going out” — it’s about having the freedom to participate in life on your own terms. We support people with mobility, anxiety, sensory needs, health appointments, social connection, errands and confidence-building.

Appointments & Essential Trips

  • GP, specialist and therapy appointments
  • NDIS meetings, reviews & provider visits
  • Medication pick-ups and health tasks
  • Bank, post office and essential errands
  • Support with paperwork (within scope)

Shopping & Daily Tasks

  • Grocery shopping & budgeting practice
  • Buying clothes, essentials & personal items
  • Planning weekly shopping lists
  • Building independence around money handling
  • Transport support & navigating stores safely

Social & Recreational Activities

  • Visiting parks, libraries or community centres
  • Going to classes, hobbies or sports programs
  • Attending faith or cultural community events
  • Joining disability-friendly social groups
  • Getting outdoors for walks and fresh air

How We Support You in the Community

1

We Start With Your Goals

You tell us what you want to work toward: confidence, independence, mobility, friendships, routines, or simply getting out of the house more.

2

We Plan Support Around You

We consider your comfort levels, sensory needs, transportation needs, mobility, cultural preferences and routines.

3

We Build Confidence Step by Step

Whether you prefer short outings or longer activities, we move at your pace and adjust based on how you’re feeling.

Who Community Access Is For

This support may be right for you if:

  • You want to attend appointments safely
  • You feel anxious or overwhelmed outside
  • You’re building independence with errands
  • You want to join programs or activities
  • You’d like to increase social connection
  • You need transport or mobility support

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you provide transport as part of community access?

Yes — support workers can transport you using their car or assist you using public transport.

Can I choose where we go?

Absolutely. Your goals and preferences guide every outing.

Do you assist people with anxiety or sensory needs?

Yes — we support participants with a wide range of emotional, sensory and mobility needs.

Is community access funded by the NDIS?

Yes, it is funded under Core Supports and sometimes Capacity Building depending on goals.

Can you attend appointments with me?

Yes — we can accompany you, provide support, help take notes (within scope) and assist with follow-up.

Can I request a support worker of a specific gender?

Yes — your comfort and cultural needs always come first.

Do you support children and young people?

Yes — we provide age-appropriate community support for children and teens.

Can you help me join social or fitness programs?

Yes — we can help research programs, make enquiries and support your attendance.

How often can I have community access?

As often as your NDIS plan allows. Many participants choose weekly or multiple times per week.

Can you support me building independence?

Yes — we encourage independence and gradual confidence-building at your pace.

Ready to Feel More Confident in the Community?

Whether you want to attend appointments, join activities, or simply get outdoors, we’re here to support you at your pace.
