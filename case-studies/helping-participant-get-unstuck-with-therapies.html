Helping a Participant Get Unstuck With Multiple Therapies

How Able Aide coordinated multiple providers, managed waitlists, clarified reports and helped the participant finally make progress.

Names changed for privacy.

Overview

“Ravi”, a 14-year-old with developmental delays and psychosocial needs, had:

  • an OT
  • a speech therapist
  • a psychologist
  • a paediatrician
  • and a behaviour support practitioner

But none of them were communicating with each other — and the family felt lost.

Challenges

  • Multiple providers with no communication
  • Reports overdue or missing
  • Therapy strategies not being used at home
  • Family overwhelmed by appointments
  • Plan review approaching with incomplete evidence

What Able Aide Did

Provider coordination

Shared updates, aligned goals and clarified responsibilities.

Tracking system

Follow-ups for reports, assessments and goals.

Family-friendly routines

Broke therapy strategies into simple daily steps.

Outcomes

  • Reports completed on time for review
  • Family understood therapy recommendations
  • Consistent progress at home and school
  • Participant more engaged in sessions
“We finally felt organised. Everything made sense again.”
