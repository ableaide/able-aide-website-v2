What we provide

What personal care & daily living support includes

Support is tailored to your goals, routines and preferences. We only provide what you want and consent to — always.

Showering & personal hygiene

Support with showering, bathing, hair washing, grooming, oral hygiene and skincare — delivered respectfully and at your preferred time and pace.

Dressing & grooming

Help with choosing and putting on clothing, managing footwear and maintaining the appearance that feels right for you — your choices, always.

Mobility & transfers

Safe support with transfers, repositioning, walking aids and movement within the home — within scope and aligned to any allied health guidance.

Meals & nutrition

Meal preparation, cooking assistance, feeding support and nutrition monitoring — culturally appropriate and built around your dietary preferences.

Medication prompts

Non-clinical reminders and prompts to support consistent medication routines — not medication administration unless appropriately covered.

Domestic assistance

Light household tasks including cleaning, laundry, tidying, vacuuming and maintaining a safe, comfortable home environment.

Morning & evening routines

Consistent, predictable support to start and end the day well — structured around your preferred routine and timing.

Continence support

Respectful, privacy-first continence care and support with continence aids — always with your consent and comfort as the priority.

Overnight & complex care

Overnight support, complex personal care and higher-intensity daily living assistance for participants with greater support needs.

Our approach

How we approach personal care

Personal care is deeply personal. We approach every interaction with the care, patience and respect it deserves.

Consent-led at every step

We never do anything without your consent. Your comfort, boundaries and preferences guide every aspect of how support is delivered — and you can change your preferences at any time.

Privacy & dignity always

We understand that personal care involves vulnerable moments. Our workers are trained to be discreet, respectful and to preserve your dignity in every interaction — no exceptions.

Consistent, familiar workers

We aim to minimise worker changes so you can build trust and familiarity with the people who support you. Consistency matters especially in personal care.

Culturally safe support

We match workers who understand your cultural background, language preferences and any faith-based considerations around personal care — particularly important for CALD communities.

How it works

How we get personal care right from the start

  1. 01

    We learn your routines & preferences

    Before any support begins, we take time to understand exactly how you like things done — morning routines, shower preferences, how you like to be spoken to, cultural considerations and what "good support" looks like for you.

  2. 02

    Careful worker matching

    We match you with workers who suit your personality, communication style, cultural background and specific care needs — prioritising consistency from the start.

  3. 03

    Support begins gently

    We introduce support workers gradually, check in regularly and adjust the approach until everything feels comfortable and right for you.

  4. 04

    Regular review & adjustment

    Your needs and preferences can change. We stay in regular contact and adjust your support plan whenever needed — with clear communication throughout.

Who it's for

Who personal care support helps

Adults with physical disability

Support with daily tasks that are difficult due to physical disability — maintaining independence, dignity and routine in your own home.

Participants with high support needs

Complex or intensive personal care for participants who require more support — including overnight care and support in SIL arrangements.

Children & young people

Age-appropriate, gentle personal care for children and young people — always with parental consent and delivered with warmth and patience.

Older participants

Dignified personal care for older NDIS participants — respectful, consistent and adapted to health conditions and mobility needs.

Participants in SIL

Personal care as part of a Supported Independent Living arrangement — coordinated with your broader SIL roster and living goals.

CALD & faith communities

Culturally safe personal care that respects language, faith-based considerations, gender preferences for workers and family values around care.
