Allied Health Access Support

Allied health services like occupational therapy, physiotherapy, speech therapy and psychology can make a huge difference in everyday life. But finding the right providers, managing waitlists and staying on top of reports can feel overwhelming.

Able Aide supports NDIS participants and families across Victoria to connect with therapists, organise assessments and put recommendations into practice.

Turning therapy plans into day-to-day support

We help bridge the gap between reports and real life — supporting you to access services and translate strategies into your everyday routines.

Talk about allied health access

Making Allied Health Easier to Navigate

It’s common for participants and families to feel lost when it comes to allied health. There are multiple disciplines, different wait times, referral pathways and NDIS requirements. We support you to connect with the right therapists, understand what they recommend, and keep everything moving.

We are not an allied health clinic ourselves. Our role is to help you access the services that best match your needs and goals — and to support the practical side of making therapy work in everyday life.

How We Support Allied Health Access

Finding the Right Providers

We help you explore and connect with allied health professionals such as:

  • Occupational Therapists (OT)
  • Physiotherapists
  • Speech Pathologists
  • Psychologists & counsellors
  • Behaviour support practitioners
  • Other NDIS-relevant specialists and assessors

Organising Appointments & Assessments

We can support you with the logistics and communication involved in allied health:

  • Making enquiries and understanding waitlists
  • Completing basic intake or referral forms (within scope)
  • Booking and confirming appointments
  • Coordinating support workers for transport or attendance
  • Helping you keep track of key dates and follow-ups

Putting Recommendations Into Practice

Therapy is most helpful when strategies are used consistently. We support with:

  • Integrating therapist recommendations into daily routines
  • Creating simple written prompts or visual supports (where appropriate)
  • Ensuring support workers understand relevant strategies
  • Supporting consistent use of equipment or aids (within scope)
  • Helping you prepare for reviews with clear information

Who This Service Is For

Allied Health Access support may be especially helpful if:

  • You’re unsure where to start with finding therapists
  • You’re on multiple waitlists and feel stuck
  • You have several therapists and feel overwhelmed by information
  • You struggle to keep track of appointments and reports
  • You need support to implement strategies at home
  • You have upcoming NDIS reviews that require new reports or assessments

Funding & Locations

  • Supports are usually funded through Capacity Building or Core (as appropriate)
  • Self-managed and plan-managed NDIS participants
  • Participants across Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Shepparton and regional Victoria

We can discuss how this support may fit within your existing funding and priorities.

Our Approach to Working With Therapists

Good allied health support is a team effort. We aim to keep communication respectful and focused on what’s best for you or your family member.

Participant-Centred

You are always at the centre of decisions. We discuss options with you, listen to your feedback and respect your preferences about which therapists you see and how often.

Collaborative

With your consent, we can liaise with your therapists, Support Coordinator, GP, school or other services to help everyone stay aligned with your goals.

Practical & Realistic

We understand that families and participants are often juggling many things. We help break recommendations into small, manageable steps that fit your real life.

Examples of Allied Health Access Support

Children & Young People

  • Linking with OT for sensory or fine-motor support
  • Arranging speech therapy for communication goals
  • Supporting families to understand and use visual schedules
  • Coordinating between school, therapists and home routines

Adults With Physical Disability

  • Linking with physio for mobility and pain management
  • Helping organise equipment assessments and trials
  • Assisting with follow-up from hospital or rehab services
  • Supporting safe use of mobility aids (within scope)

Participants With Mental Health Needs

  • Connecting with psychologists or counsellors where appropriate
  • Supporting routines that align with therapeutic strategies
  • Coordinating with other supports to reduce overwhelm
  • Providing calm, consistent support workers alongside therapy

Allied Health Access – Frequently Asked Questions

Are you an allied health clinic?

No. Able Aide is not an allied health clinic. We are a disability support provider that helps NDIS participants access and coordinate allied health services provided by therapists.

Can you recommend specific therapists?

We can suggest options based on your location, needs and preferences, and share information to help you decide. We aim to remain transparent and participant-focused in all referrals.

Can you book appointments for me?

Yes, we can help making and confirming bookings, and coordinating them with your other supports and commitments, where funded and appropriate.

Do you come to therapy sessions with me?

Where it’s helpful and within your funding, a support worker or Support Coordinator may be able to attend some sessions or reviews with you. We will discuss what feels right for you.

Can you help with NDIS reports?

We can help you keep track of which reports are needed and when, remind you to request them, and communicate with providers about timelines. Therapists remain responsible for writing the clinical report itself.

Is this a separate service from Support Coordination?

It can be. Some allied health access tasks may be part of Support Coordination; others may be delivered as part of core or capacity supports. We can discuss how it fits best in your plan.

What if I’m already seeing therapists?

We can help you organise information, coordinate between providers and put existing recommendations into practice. The goal is to reduce overwhelm and help your supports work together.

Do you help with equipment and assistive technology?

We assist with the practical side, such as attending assessments and following up on recommendations. Prescribing and clinical decisions remain with your therapist.

Can you help if English is not my first language?

Yes. We will always do our best to work with interpreters, bilingual workers (if available) and culturally appropriate services.

How do I get started?

You can contact us directly, speak with your Support Coordinator, or ask a provider to send a referral. We’ll talk through your goals, funding and next steps.

Need Help Accessing Therapy or Assessments?

If you feel stuck, overwhelmed or unsure where to start with allied health, we’re here to guide you gently through the process.
