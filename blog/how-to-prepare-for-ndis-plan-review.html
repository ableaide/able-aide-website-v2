How to Prepare for an NDIS Plan Review

Plan reviews (reassessments) can feel stressful — but with some gentle preparation, they can become an opportunity to get supports that truly match your needs, routines and goals.

Need help preparing?

Able Aide can help you reflect on your current supports, gather information and feel more confident going into your plan review.

1. Understand what an NDIS plan review is

An NDIS plan review (now often called a “plan reassessment”) is when the NDIS looks at your current supports, goals and needs to decide what your next plan should include.

The key question they’re asking is: “Are your current supports helping you work towards your goals, or do they need to change?”

2. Look back: what has worked — and what hasn’t?

Before your plan review, it helps to reflect on the past 6–12 months. You don’t need a long report — simple dot points are enough.

  • Which supports helped you the most?
  • Were there supports you didn’t use — and why?
  • Any changes in your health, living situation or family?
  • Did you feel you had enough support hours?
  • Do your current goals still fit, or do they need updating?

You can use your own notes or simple worksheets from our Resources page.

3. Gather reports and letters from providers

Evidence from therapists, support workers, doctors and coordinators helps the NDIS understand:

  • Why you need certain supports
  • What has changed since your last plan
  • What may happen if supports are reduced

Types of helpful evidence:

  • Therapy reports (OT, physio, speech, psychology)
  • Behaviour support plans or updates
  • Letters from GPs or specialists
  • Progress summaries
  • Examples of day-to-day challenges

Request reports 4–6 weeks before your review whenever possible.

4. Be clear about your goals

Goals do not need to sound complicated — they simply need to reflect what matters to you and where you want support.

Examples:

  • “I want to build more independence with showering and dressing.”
  • “I want to feel confident attending appointments.”
  • “I want my child to participate more at kinder and home routines.”
  • “I want a small, consistent support team that understands my anxiety.”

5. Think about what you need more of, less of or different

Ask yourself:

  • Do I need more of what is working?
  • Do I need less of what I’m not using?
  • Do I need different supports?
  • Do I need help coordinating everything?

A Support Coordinator can help you think this through calmly and clearly.

6. Plan what to say in the meeting

Many people feel nervous about NDIS meetings. Preparing a few key points can help you feel more confident.

  • 1–3 major changes since your last plan
  • 1–3 things that are still challenging
  • Examples of how supports help day-to-day
  • Any safety, mental health or behaviour concerns

7. Common mistakes to avoid

  • Under-reporting challenges because you don’t want to sound negative
  • Saying “everything is fine” when it isn’t
  • Not sharing changes in family, housing or health
  • Waiting too late to request reports

8. When to ask for help

Many people need support preparing for NDIS reviews — it’s completely normal.

  • Reflecting on what’s working
  • Gathering reports and evidence
  • Linking goals to the supports you need
  • Feeling more confident speaking with the NDIS

You can contact us for a gentle, no-pressure chat about how we can support you.
